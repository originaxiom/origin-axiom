\begin{abstract}
The question of why the universe exists — why there is something rather than absolute nothingness — has long eluded a principled resolution in physics. We introduce the Origin Axiom: the universe is structurally forbidden from perfect global cancellation, enforced on complex scalar amplitudes $A(\mathcal{C})$ over configuration space, preventing $|A| \to 0$. This axiom, grounded in a unifying phase $\thetastar$ extracted from Standard Model flavor observables (PMNS and CKM matrices, fiducial 3.63 rad, band [2.18, 5.54]), provides a meta-principle that bridges disparate scales of physics.

Through systematic, reproducible simulations, we demonstrate how $\thetastar$-modulated seesaw neutrino masses generate a vacuum energy shift in microcavity models ($\sim$2.2\% modulation), which in turn influences cosmological expansion in an FRW framework ($\sim$1\% acceleration in scale factor growth), while stabilizing microstructure in 3D lattices with defects as particle-like structures. The axiom ensures stability across all scales, with emergent implications including flavor-linked dark energy and dark matter candidates. The framework is fully reproducible via open-source code at \url{https://github.com/originaxiom/origin-axiom} and \url{https://github.com/originaxiom/origin-axiom-theta-star}.
\end{abstract}
