## The acceptance discipline (so we neither bypass nor over-claim)

A candidate "ingredient emerged from interaction" is accepted only if it is: **(1) structure, not value-matching**
(no fitting observed constants); **(2) null-passing** (a coincidence/generic baseline is rejected); **(3) robust**
(≥2 seeds / ≥2 models / θ-averaged, per the index-method lesson `B178`); **(4) falsifiable** (we state what would
*disqualify* it); **(5) firewall-clean** (emergent, one-way-cited, nothing to `CLAIMS.md`). The same five gates that
caught the density trap, the model-switch, and the φ-privilege over-read this session — now applied to the *target*.

Based on everything banked, the **likely** outcome is that more *content* ingredients also relocate external (the wall

## The first hunt — DONE (`B181`/V175): the inversion

**Large-N / criticality** asked: does a phase transition give the collective an emergent scale? **Answer: an
inversion.** The metallic quasicrystal is **permanently critical** — Lyapunov `γ≈0` on the spectrum at *all* coupling
(no metal–insulator transition, vs the Aubry–André control which localizes at `λ>2`). Permanent criticality `⟹`
localization length `ξ→∞` `⟹` **scale-invariant** `⟹` scale-free. So criticality **explains** the object's
scale-freeness rather than providing a scale; a finite emergent length needs *breaking* criticality (off the metallic
class) and is **dimensionless** (lattice units → external). The most-cited scale-door is thus closed for this object;
the scale lives on the **Hitchin/Higgs (external)** side (`K018`/`B169`).
