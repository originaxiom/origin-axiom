## The frame

The metallic trace-map tower is governed by **two distinct symmetries**, and the open questions sort cleanly by
which one they belong to:

| symmetry | type | what it runs | banked in |
|---|---|---|---|
| **bulk opposition involution `θ=−w₀`** | `ℤ/2` | the tower's **sign / multiplicity** structure — the closed form `⌈(n−h)/2⌉`/`⌊(n−h)/2⌋` over the height-`h` root spaces | B62, **B111** |
| **peripheral cusp-spectrum symmetry** | `ℤ/k` | the **degree=rank** data — the longitude `L=c·Mⁿ`, the per-component scalar `c`, the exponent `k` | B76, B95, B106 |

**degree=rank lives at their interface.** B111 made this literal: the proved tower **is** the bulk-`θ` closed form
**with exactly one `char(M)` promoted to `char(Mⁿ)`** — and that single promotion *is* the interface (the meridian
`M`, a bulk generator, becomes the longitude `Mⁿ`, a peripheral object). The bulk symmetry supplies everything
except the top power; the peripheral symmetry supplies the top power.

## How it organizes the open work

- **The tower (sign half):** bulk-`θ`. Largely **closed-form** now (B111) — the `ρ_n` proof's sign structure is
  the opposition-involution multiplicity law.
- **degree=rank's `c` (peripheral half):** the `ℤ/k` cusp spectrum (`S022` lead). B108 proved `θ` (bulk, `ℤ/2`)
  cannot reach the order-4 `c=i`; B111 proved the cusp arithmetic controls the exponent (`M⁴`-scalar forces `k=3`
  on the secondary). The missing ingredient is peripheral.
- **The `ρ_n` prize:** needs **both** halves — the bulk-`θ` multiplicities (closed form) **and** why exactly one
  `char(M)→char(Mⁿ)` (degree=rank, peripheral). The frame says the proof is a *join* of the two symmetries, not a
  single mechanism.

## What this is and is not

It **is** the lens that explains why the single-mechanism attempts failed (`θ→c` B108; `s_n↔c`, `ord−1` B111):
each tried to run the *peripheral* `c` off the *bulk* `θ`. It **is not** a claim that the two symmetries combine
in any specific proved way — that combination (the covering-degree mechanism, `S022`) is an open hypothesis. The
frame guides *where to look*; the verdicts live in `../frontier/` and the `V`-ledger.
