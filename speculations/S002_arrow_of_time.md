# S002 — arrow of time = the trace-map flow direction

**Status: `SUPPORTED` (structure); D2 pending.** Firewalled; not a claim.

**Structural fact (cited).** The three trace-map fixed-point classes have distinct stability (B106): the **trivial**
rep is a repeller, the **geometric** a saddle, the **Dehn-filling** partially elliptic. The flow trivial→geometric
is **one-way**. Separately, Dickson parity (V22) makes monodromy-inversion `N→N⁻¹` twist the odd sectors by `(−1)`,
and the forced `det=−1` slice carries an orientation factor `(t+1)` the `det=+1` slice lacks (B92–B94).

**[LEAP] (kept separate).** Time's arrow = the **unstable direction of the scale-RG**; irreversibility = stretching
along the unstable manifold (positive Kolmogorov–Sinai entropy). The void (trivial rep) is the low-entropy past;
the flow runs away from it. The `(−1)ᵏ` parity asymmetry is the orientation.
