## The frame — "form is what survives symmetrization; value is what breaks it"
Three of this arc's results say the *same structural thing* in three languages:
- **The hierarchy** reduces (`../frontier/B327`, `B329`) to whether the arithmetic `2T↪E₆` is **self-dual (σ-stable)**
  or **chiral (non-σ-stable)**. Every *natural* embedding is σ-stable → `n₁=n₂` → the light generations stay degenerate.
  The splitting (a *value*) would require a chiral choice the object does not supply.
- **The CP sign** is a **Galois pair** `√−3 → −√−3` (`B318`); the **no-forced-choice** mechanism (`../frontier/B330`)
  shows *every* trace-map-invariant discrete invariant examined is a symmetrizable Galois orbit — the object gives the
  orbit, never a member.
- **The ℤ/3-breaking** is **finite congruence torsion** `(ℤ/4)²` with an **irreducible** deck action (`../frontier/B326`)
  — *no invariant line*, i.e. no distinguished sub-object to be forced; maximally symmetric.

**[HOOK] The unifying reading:** the object is a machine for producing *symmetric structure* — Galois orbits, self-dual
restrictions, irreducible modules, balanced `3/3̄` pairings. A **value** (a mass ratio, a CP sign, a chirality) is
exactly a **member picked out of a symmetric set** — a *broken* symmetry. The object forces the set; the **seam** (the
closing / Dehn filling / the interaction with the nothing, `../frontier/B286`) is where a member is chosen. *Form =

## [MATH] one new grounded observation — the two ends are the two ramifications of one polynomial
`Δ(t)=t²−3t+1` (the figure-eight Alexander polynomial) carries both arithmetic ends *as its reduction behaviour*
(verified, `../frontier/B326` bonus + here):

| reduction | behaviour | end |
|---|---|---|
| `Δ mod 2` | `t²+t+1 = Φ₃` (inert) | **√−3** (Eisenstein; appears mod 2 because `−3 ≡ 1 mod 4`) |
| `disc Δ = 5`, `Δ mod 5` | `(t+1)²` — **ramified** (double root) | **√5** (golden; ramifies at its own prime) |
| `Δ mod 11, 19, …` | splits | primes split in `ℚ(√5)` |

So the golden end is the **ramification** of `Δ` at `p=5`, and the Eisenstein end is its **mod-2 reduction to `Φ₃`** —
one degree-2 object, its two ends visible as two arithmetic behaviours. **[HOOK]** the "two-ended object" is literally the
two reduction-types of `Δ`. (Prior-art R7: APPEARS-NOVEL as a stated coincidence but *light* — `−3≡1 mod 4` makes the
mod-2 identity automatic; keep as `[HOOK]`, not a theorem.)
