## The one-page verdict

The literature is cleanly two-tier on "what fixes the SM":

- **FORCED (theorem-level):** anomaly cancellation → the hypercharges (Diophantine; the
  SM's *only* computed value-level structure); Witten's ℤ/2 (even # of SU(2) doublets);
  the centralizer-of-holonomy → the *unbroken* subgroup (Hosotani/Wilson-line); the
  Langlands pairing (G,ᴸG) once G exists (Kapustin–Witten).
- **CHOSEN (model-builder input, everywhere):** the global form of the group; **three
  generations** (derived nowhere); the GUT group, Higgs reps, VEV directions, breaking
  chains; **all couplings, masses, mixings**.

**The gauge GROUP is cheap** — three independent mechanisms (ADE singularity type /
McKay; NCG spectral-triple algebra; Jordan-stabilizer) all land on SU(3)×SU(2)×U(1), so a
group-level match clears no firewall bar. **VALUES are never resident** in a geometric
object — the one falsifiable value prediction ever made (NCG Higgs at 170 GeV) *failed*
(2012) and was patched. This independently **corroborates the three-level negative** and
the observer-coupling reorientation.

**The project's actual gap.** Every gauge-adjacent structure in the object (E₆→F₄, the
ℤ/11 charge, the orientation ℤ/2) has been assessed only at the **bookkeeping pole** —
group actions and gradings, with **no anomaly, β-function, or loop observable ever
computed**. Those are the discrete, exact, in-sandbox tests that decide whether a symmetry
is *load-bearing* rather than a relabeling.

## Gauge-vs-bookkeeping diagnostic checklist (Elitzur, 't Hooft, GWP, Nielsen–Ninomiya…)

1. **Propagation** (Elitzur): does anything carry its own kinetic term / radiate, or is it a relabeling?
2. **Anomaly content** ('t Hooft matching): a bookkeeping grading has none; a nonzero, RG-matched anomaly is positive evidence — *discrete, exact, best-suited to this project*.
3. **β-function sign** (asymptotic freedom vs screening): a static grading has no coupling to run.
4. **Confinement order parameter** (Wilson area vs perimeter; Fredenhagen–Marcu).
5. **Mass gap**. 6. **Chirality rigidity** (Nielsen–Ninomiya: chiral+gauged can't latticize without doublers). 7. **Goldstone vs Higgs** (Elitzur forbids literal gauge SSB). 8. **Weinberg–Witten** (no emergent massless charged spin>½).
