## The question

there a canonical Gal(ℚ(√5)/ℚ)-equivariant bijection between the measurement/
irrep torsor {golden irreps of 2I} and the MTC torsor {Fibonacci, Yang–Lee}

  invariant (so a distinguished element on each side matches), giving a

- **OBSTRUCTED (merely same-σ)**: the two sides carry DIFFERENT amounts of
  canonical structure — one torsor is canonically POINTED, the other is
  genuinely symmetric (no basepoint) — so no canonical iso; "measurement =
  fiber functor" is a same-σ Galois-torsor realization, NOT a canonical
  identity. cc2's caution confirmed, with the concrete obstruction named.

3. If (1) has no basepoint and (2) does ⇒ OBSTRUCTED, and the obstruction =
   the observer-coupling asymmetry (the object's category is unitary-pointed;
