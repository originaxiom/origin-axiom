## Task 4 — degree=rank (`Mⁿ = L`, longitude = root-of-unity · meridian^rank): apparently new

**The component structure is confirmed in the literature** (and matches B71 exactly): **Heusener–Muñoz–Porti**,
*The `SL(3,ℂ)`-character variety of the figure eight knot* (arXiv:1505.04451, Illinois J. Math. 60, 2016) — the
variety has **five components of dim 2**: totally reducible, partially reducible, and **three irreducible** — one
*distinguished* (containing the `Sym²: SL(2,ℂ)→SL(3,ℂ)` curve = our **V0**), and **two induced by exceptional
Dehn fillings** (= our **W1/W2**). Also Falbel–Guilloux–Koseleff–Rouillier–Thistlethwaite (arXiv:1412.4711) and
Falbel et al. PGL(3,ℂ) (arXiv:1307.6697, "three conjugacy classes of unipotent boundary holonomy"). The
meridian/longitude **eigenvalues** appear as the enhanced-Ptolemy variables `m_{i,j}, l_{i,j}` in **Zickert**,
*Ptolemy coordinates, Dehn invariant and the A-polynomial* (arXiv:1405.0025).

**But the specific power relation `Mⁿ = L`** — the longitude eigenvalue equal to a *fixed power* (`= rank`) of the
meridian eigenvalue, up to a root of unity (our B71/B83/B89/B106 degree=rank) — **was not found stated in the
published literature** as of this search. The Ptolemy framework *computes* the A-varieties and carries the
eigenvalues, but does not single out the power law; surgery relations of the form `D = X − L^{−n}` use a different
`n` (the filling coefficient), not the rank. **Recorded as "apparently new (2026-06-07)"** — a priority item for
Paper 1's novelty claim. (Verify-don't-trust caveat: a negative literature search is not a proof of novelty;
flagged for a specialist read before any published novelty claim.)

## Task 5 — the n=4 / W₄ truncation (S010): the anchor exists, but does NOT single out n=4

**Yes, there is a real, named condition** — and it is citable. In 3d higher-spin gravity the gauge algebra
`hs[λ]` **truncates to `sl(N)` at `λ = N`**, giving gauge fields of spin `s = 2, 3, …, N`; the asymptotic symmetry
is the **`W_N` algebra**. So **`sl(4)` Chern–Simons = spin-(2,3,4) higher-spin gravity with `W₄` asymptotic
symmetry** (Campoleoni–Fredenhagen–Pfenninger–Theisen; **"Aspects of three-dimensional spin-4 gravity"**, JHEP
02(2012)035 / arXiv:1111.2834; general hs[λ]→sl(N) truncation, arXiv:1304.0258).

**The honest yes/no, though:** the truncation at spin `N` is just *"choose `𝔤 = sl(N)`"* — it is the generic
`sl(N) ↔ W_N` correspondence, **not** an independent condition that *distinguishes* `N = 4`. So the W₄ structure
is **real and citable**, but it does **not** independently support the dimensionality pointer (`n=4 → 3+1`): there
is no spin-4 *cutoff condition* forcing `N=4`; one may equally pick any `N`. **S010 result:** the W₄ anchor
exists (cite arXiv:1111.2834), but it does **not** single out n=4 — the dimensionality reading gains a citation,
not a forcing. (The genuine `n=4` threshold remains the degree=rank wall at `n=5`, B95, an *internal* fact, not
the W₄ truncation.)
