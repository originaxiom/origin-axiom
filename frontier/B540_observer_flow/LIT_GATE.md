## RESULT 2 — the observer flow (B540): **KNOWN**

Iterating the return-word derivation operator and finding its fixed
points/cycles is not new — it is the **literal founding motivation of
derived-sequence theory**:
- **Rauzy (~1992)** observed (blackboard, recorded in Durand's memorial paper
  arXiv:1111.0650) that iterating return-word coding on the Fibonacci fixed
  point recovers the same sequence, and asked whether the iteration is always
  eventually periodic.
- **Durand 1998** (Thm 5: primitive-substitutive ⟺ finitely many derived
  sequences) + the composition law Prop 6(5) make eventual periodicity a
  near-immediate pigeonhole fact.
- **"Closed under derivation"** (Košík–Starosta 2019; Pelantová–Starosta)
  already exhibits derivation FIXED POINTS (the Tribonacci singleton {τ}) and
  small invariant families with an explicit iterated operator Δ proven
  eventually periodic (an explicit period-6 cycle).

**Consequence:** B540's "observer flow — σ a fixed point, one 2-cycle" is a
NEW COMPUTED INSTANCE (4-letter) of an ESTABLISHED phenomenon, **not a new
construction**. The math stands; the "novel observer-flow theory" framing is
RETIRED. Cite Rauzy/Durand 1998/Košík–Starosta. The B540 double-clock 2-cycle
is a specific instance; its VALUE is the concrete 12-node structure and the
ℤ/2 reading, not novelty.
