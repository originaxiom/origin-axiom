WHY NONE SUCCEED:

The kill is an existence proof: golden-field Pisot coupling matrices exist
at dim 5. The B742 recompute confirms this with exact arithmetic (two
distinct sextic Pisot witnesses, 46656 couplings scanned). The face's
golden parameters (phi^3, 1/(phi*sqrt(5))) are sibling consequences of
the same golden ratio / Fibonacci matrix, not constraints on the coupling
scan's outcomes.

For the face to be relevant, a theorem would need to show that m004's
spectral data (Laplacian eigenvalues, scattering matrix, or mixing
structure) RESTRICTS which F-couplings can have Pisot dominant eigenvalues.
The Selberg trace formula connects the Laplacian spectrum to the length
spectrum (which encodes monodromy data), but this connection goes from
monodromy -> spectral data. The monodromy F^2 is already fully known
algebraically; the spectral data is a consequence of it, not a constraint
on the algebraic properties of F-coupling block matrices.

RESIDUAL RISK (the sharpest open question):
If m004's DISCRETE newform spectrum carries golden-field eigenvalues (the
open question flagged at B746 door #2), AND those eigenvalues constrain
Fibonacci-coupling Pisot existence through a currently unknown theorem,
the irrelevance verdict falls. Both conditionals are open and neither can
be computationally resolved with available tools (Hejhal-class machinery
required for the first; no known mathematics bears on the second).
