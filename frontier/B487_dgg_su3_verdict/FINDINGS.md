## Why the SM identification FAILS (four decisive points)
1. **FLAVOR, not GAUGE.** The SU(3) is a GLOBAL FLAVOR symmetry of the 3d theory — no gauge
   bosons, no gluons, no confinement, no color force. The SM's SU(3) is a GAUGED color
   symmetry. A global symmetry and a gauge symmetry are categorically different objects. The
   ONLY gauge symmetry of T[4₁] is the abelian **U(1)** it is built on.
2. **3d, not 4d.** T[4₁] is a 3-dimensional N=2 theory. The Standard Model is a 4-dimensional
   gauge theory. Different spacetime dimension.
3. **One symmetry, not three factors.** The enhancement is **U(1)_flavor → SU(3)_flavor**:
   the SU(2)×U(1) seat-1 cites is the UV-visible SUBGROUP of the single SU(3) that emerges in
   the IR (SU(2)×U(1) ⊂ SU(3)). It is not a product SU(3)×SU(2)×U(1); there is ONE flavor
   symmetry, SU(3), whose unenhanced form looks like SU(2)×U(1). Counting them as three
   independent factors is double-counting the same symmetry.
4. **No SM matter content.** Two chirals of U(1) charge +1 are not the SM's chiral fermion
   representations (quarks/leptons in (3,2), (3̄,1), (1,2), … with hypercharges). The
   structure test — right representations, right chirality, right matter — is not met.
