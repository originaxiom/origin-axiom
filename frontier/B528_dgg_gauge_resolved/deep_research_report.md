# Deep-research: the higher-rank 3d-3d gauge group (2026-07-12)

## Summary
Across every standard construction of the 3d-3d correspondence, the 3d N=2 theory T_N[M] attached to a hyperbolic 3-manifold (e.g. the figure-eight complement) has an ABELIAN gauge group — a product of U(1) Chern-Simons-matter factors — at N=2/SL(2,C) (Dimofte-Gaiotto-Gukov), at all higher rank K>=3 (Dimofte-Gabella-Goncharov K-decomposition), and in the Terashima-Yamazaki cluster-algebra formalism. The nonabelian SL(N,C)/PGL(K,C) is the STRUCTURE GROUP of the flat connection / the gauge group of complex Chern-Simons theory ON M — a distinct object the correspondence RELATES to T_N[M], not the 3d QFT gauge group; DGG explicitly state that K "does not appear as the rank of a gauge group." There is NO "U(N-1) nonabelian at rank N" ladder: for a single ideal tetrahedron a gauge group first appears only at K>=5 and is still abelian (U(1)^2), so the claim "higher-rank 3d-3d gives nonabelian gauge" is FALSE as stated — it is a gauge-vs-structure-group conflation. The one genuine exception is the DEFECT setting, where Gang-Kim-Romo-Yamazaki give a nonabelian description of T_N[M] (built from the T[SU(N)] domain-wall quiver U(1)xU(2)x...xU(N-1)) needed to see codimension-4 defects labeled by SU(N) representations, tied to a Higgsing prescription. Separately, Cordova-Jafferis show the M5-brane reduction yields complex nonabelian Chern-Simons — but that is the structure-group/CS side, not the T[M] gauge group.

## Findings (adversarially verified, primary sources)

### (a) VERDICT: ABELIAN. For N=2 (SL(2,C)), the DGG theory T[M] is an abelian Chern-Simons-matter theory: each ideal tetrahedron is one chiral multiplet with background CS terms, and gluing gauges a U(1) flavor symmetry per internal edge (plus an edge superpotential). The gauge group is a product of U(1)'s; for the figure-eight complement (2 tetrahedra, 1 cusp) it is a single dynamical U(1) coupled to two charge-+1 chirals, matching gauge rank ~ (#tetrahedra - #cusps) = 1.
- confidence: high | vote: unanimous 3-0 across all supporting claims [0,1,2,3,8,9,23,24]
- sources: arXiv:1108.4389 (Dimofte-Gaiotto-Gukov, Comm. Math. Phys. 325 (2014) 367); arXiv:1803.04009 (Gang-Yonekura, JHEP 07 (2018) 145); arXiv:2603.05236 (Chung, 2026)
- evidence: DGG state verbatim they describe T_M 'as the IR fixed point of an abelian Chern-Simons-matter theory'; tetrahedron = 'essentially the theory of a single chiral multiplet', glued by 'gauging some flavor symmetries... and adding a superpotential coupling for each internal edge'. Gang-Yonekura give the figure-eight theory explicitly (eq. 1.3): 'A U(1)_0 vector multiplet coupled to two chiral multiplets of charge +1', with only a U(1) dynamical gauge field. Chung (2026) restates: 'Each ideal tetrahedron corresponds to a chiral multiplet with background Chern-Simons terms in 3d N=2 abelian theories and the gluing operation is related to the SL(2,Z) action.'

### (b) VERDICT: ABELIAN (no nonabelian ladder). At higher rank K>=3, the K-decomposition theories T_K[M] are STILL abelian Chern-Simons-matter theories ('just as for K=2'); higher K adds more U(1) factors and chirals (DOF grow cubically in K), never nonabelian gauge symmetry. Concretely for a single ideal tetrahedron: K=2 is 1 free chiral (U(1) flavor, no gauge), K=3 is 4 free chirals (U(1)^4 flavor, no gauge), K=4 is 10 chirals + degree-six W (U(1)^9 flavor, no gauge), and a gauge group appears only at K>=5 — and even then it is abelian U(1)^2. There is NO U(2)@K=3 or U(3)@K=4, i.e. no U(N-1) nonabelian ladder.
- confidence: high | vote: 3-0 on [6,10,12,13]; core abelian statement [4] was 2-1 but corroborated by unanimous companions
- sources: arXiv:1301.0192 (Dimofte-Gabella-Goncharov, JHEP 11 (2016) 151)
- evidence: Abstract, verbatim: 'Just as for K=2, the theories T_K[M] are described as IR fixed points of abelian Chern-Simons-matter theories' and 'In the large K limit... the degrees of freedom of T_K[M] grow cubically in K' (body: (1/6)N K(K^2-1) chirals). Table (1.6) gives the single-tetrahedron ladder verbatim (K=2 U(1); K=3 U(1)^4; K=4 U(1)^9; K=5 '20 chirals, U(1)^2 gauge group'); flavor rank is U(1)^{(K-1)^2}, an abelian product, NOT a nonabelian U(K-1). The paper: 'for all K>=5, the gluing rules force T_K[Delta] to have both a nontrivial superpotential and a nontrivial gauge group' — but that gauge group is abelian.

### (c) VERDICT: DISTINCTION CLARIFIED — 'U(N-1) nonabelian at rank N' is a gauge-vs-structure-group CONFLATION. The nonabelian SL(N,C)/PGL(K,C) is the STRUCTURE GROUP of the framed flat connection / the gauge group of complexified Chern-Simons theory ON M (the character-variety / Coulomb-branch-moduli target). It is a separate object that the correspondence RELATES to — does not identify with — the 3d N=2 gauge group of T_N[M], which is abelian. DGG explicitly note K is NOT the rank of a 3d gauge group.
- confidence: high | vote: unanimous 3-0 across [5,7,11,14,15,19,22]
- sources: arXiv:1301.0192 (Dimofte-Gabella-Goncharov); arXiv:1510.05011 (Gang-Kim-Romo-Yamazaki, JHEP 10 (2016) 062); arXiv:1702.05045 (Alday-Benetti Genolini-Bullimore-van Loon, JHEP 04 (2017) 170); arXiv:1305.2891 (Cordova-Jafferis, JHEP 11 (2017) 119)
- evidence: DGG, verbatim: 'Mathematically, we study framed flat PGL(K,C)-connections on... M' and 'K does not appear as a continuously tunable parameter in T_K[M], such as the rank of a gauge group. (This is obvious, for example, in (1.6).)' Gang-Kim-Romo-Yamazaki, verbatim: 'The... 3d-3d correspondence is a relation between complexified Chern-Simons theory (with gauge group SL(N,C)) on M and a 3d N=2 theory T_N[M]' — a relation between two distinct objects. Alday et al.: the Coulomb branch of T[M] is 'the moduli space of SL(N,C) flat connections... captured by a Chern-Simons theory with complex gauge group SL(N,C)'. Cordova-Jafferis derive complex nonabelian CS from the M5 reduction, confirming SL(N,C) lives on the CS/structure side.

### (e) VERDICT: MIXED — one genuinely nonabelian handle exists, but only in the DEFECT setting, not the generic defect-free construction. Gang-Kim-Romo-Yamazaki 'highlight the non-Abelian description of the 3d N=2 T_N[M] theory with defect included', built from the T[SU(N)] domain-wall theory (a quiver U(1)xU(2)x...xU(N-1), nonabelian for N>=3) required because codimension-4 defects are labeled by SU(N) representations an abelian description cannot see; a Higgsing prescription relates it back to the abelian description. By contrast, Terashima-Yamazaki's cluster-algebra construction (which covers the 6d A_{N-1} theory on link complements for general N and reproduces the SL(N) CS partition function) is explicitly ABELIAN ('we specialize to Abelian gauge theories'). So no defect-free construction of a 1-cusped hyperbolic complement is known to give a genuinely nonabelian 3d gauge group.
- confidence: high | vote: unanimous 3-0 on [16,17,21,22]
- sources: arXiv:1510.05011 (Gang-Kim-Romo-Yamazaki, JHEP 10 (2016) 062); arXiv:1301.5902 (Terashima-Yamazaki, PTEP 2014 023B01); arXiv:1305.2891 (Cordova-Jafferis)
- evidence: Gang-Kim-Romo-Yamazaki abstract, verbatim: 'We also highlight the non-Abelian description of the 3d N=2 T_N[M] theory with defect included, as well as its Higgsing prescription and the resulting refinement in complex CS theory' — a genuine gauge-theoretic (T[SU(N)] quiver) handle, scoped to defects. Terashima-Yamazaki, verbatim: 'we specialize to Abelian gauge theories in this paper', with U(1) symmetries labeled by a set (some flavor, some gauge) and gauging = integrating over sigma_i; abstract confirms general-N coverage of 6d A_{N-1} on link complements matching the holomorphic SL(N) CS partition function. Cordova-Jafferis' nonabelian object is complex CS on M (structure side), not the T[M] gauge group.

### (d) Framework-generic vs manifold-specific: the ABELIAN character of the 3d gauge group is FRAMEWORK-GENERIC (holds for all M and all K in DGG/K-decomposition/TY); what is manifold-specific is only the abelian gauge RANK (set by the triangulation — roughly #internal-edges / #tetrahedra minus cusp data, e.g. rank 1 for the figure-eight). The 'U(N-1) at rank N' pattern is not manifold-specific — it does not occur for ANY hyperbolic 3-manifold; per single tetrahedron the gauge group is at most abelian U(1)^2 (K=5), and the only K-dependence is more U(1)'s and chirals (cubic in K).
- confidence: medium | vote: supported by 3-0 claims [6,13] on the per-tetrahedron/framework-generic side; manifold-specificity of the rank is inferred, not separately quoted
- sources: arXiv:1301.0192 (Dimofte-Gabella-Goncharov); arXiv:1108.4389 (Dimofte-Gaiotto-Gukov)
- evidence: The abelian statement in DGG (K=2) and DGGon (all K) is made framework-wide, not manifold-by-manifold. The single-tetrahedron table (1.6) is the framework-generic building block; gluing produces manifold-specific numbers of U(1)'s and chirals (cubic-in-K growth) but never changes the abelian nature. No source exhibits a U(N-1) nonabelian gauge group for any specific manifold; the rank itself (e.g. U(1) for figure-eight) is what varies with M.

### Supporting context — DGG incompleteness reinforces (not contradicts) the abelian verdict. The DGG triangulation theory T_DGG[M] is INCOMPLETE: it systematically misses certain branches of flat connections, including even the abelian (reducible, maximal-torus) flat connections — but 'abelian' here refers to abelian FLAT CONNECTIONS on the geometry/structure side, NOT to the 3d gauge group being abelian. The complete theory must realize all G_C flat connections; recent work reproduces missing branches while keeping the 3d theory an abelian half-index/CS-matter.
- confidence: high | vote: 3-0 on [18,19]; [20] was 2-1 but corroborated
- sources: arXiv:1405.3663 (Chung-Dimofte-Gukov-Sulkowski, JHEP 04 (2016) 140); arXiv:2603.05236 (Chung, 2026)
- evidence: 1405.3663, verbatim: 'certain branches of flat connections are always missing... Examples of such lost branches include even the simplest flat connections on M3, namely the abelian ones (i.e. flat connections that can be conjugated to the maximal torus of G_C)', and 'the complete theory T[M3;G] must realize all G_C flat connections on M3'. This uses 'abelian/non-abelian' as a property of the SL(N,C) character-variety branches, exactly the structure-group side — a clean illustration that 'non-abelian' in this literature is about flat connections, not the 3d gauge group. Chung (2026) resolves the missing abelian branches within an abelian U(1) half-index.
