# FRONTIER CAMPAIGN — CHARGE, OBSERVER, MEASUREMENT

## Purpose

Push the Level-1 substitution beyond broad analogies and toward exact theorems,
complete finite classifications, and externally testable predictions.

The campaign proceeds one cell at a time. A later cell cannot inherit an
interpretation from an earlier cell unless the relevant mathematical map has
been explicitly constructed.

Repository baseline at launch:

```text
0377952e7c7d2313232fdcd3592c303317a71309
PC22 dark-hyperbola letter + paper-portfolio read
```

No repository writes are made during exploration. Integration follows only after
independent verification and a frozen handoff.

---

# Governance

Every cell has five stages:

1. **SYNC** — refresh trunk and identify duplication or corrections.
2. **PREREGISTER** — freeze question, predictions, controls, and kill conditions.
3. **COMPUTE** — exact arithmetic first; certified numerics only when exact closure is unavailable.
4. **ADJUDICATE** — label every claim:
   - `PROVED EXACTLY`
   - `CERTIFIED NUMERICALLY`
   - `COMPUTED IN A FINITE RANGE`
   - `CONJECTURED`
   - `REFUTED`
5. **PACKAGE** — report, structured certificate, reproducer, tests, and tables.

A negative result closes its proposed mechanism but does not erase any weaker theorem
proved along the way.

# Cell order

## CELL 1 — Universal Z/11 Charge Transport

### Question

Does the unique torsion charge of the base substitution survive every canonical
observer in the 12-node observer flow, and does the unique two-cycle act
nontrivially on it?

### Exact targets

- Compute `SNF(I-A)` for every observer matrix.
- Compute the fixed left charge space over `F_11`.
- Construct every return-word Parikh intertwiner `P`.
- Verify:
  \[
  A_s P = P A_t.
  \]
- Transport:
  \[
  \chi_t=\chi_s P.
  \]
- Compute cycle holonomy in `F_11^×`.

### Kill conditions

- Any observer has no nonzero mod-11 fixed charge.
- Any edge sends the charge to zero.
- Charge transport depends on arbitrary canonical relabeling.
- The two-cycle holonomy is not invariant under node rescaling.

### Status

`COMPLETE` in the launch run.

## CELL 2 — Observer-Flow Closure Theorem

### Question

Why are there exactly 12 systems, three fixed points, and one nontrivial two-cycle
under the prefix-return observation rule?

### Work

- Recompute closure without finite-prefix assumptions.
- Replace long-word discovery by bounded return-automaton arguments.
- Classify nodes by:
  \[
  (q,\;F_2^4\text{ phase},\;Z/11\text{ charge},\;
    \operatorname{SNF}(I-A),\;\mathrm{GL}(Z)\text{ class}).
  \]
- Test protocol dependence using equally natural prefix windows of lengths 1, 2,
  and minimal bispecial closures.

### Kill conditions

- The graph expands under the same rule at greater certified depth.
- Equally natural observation rules produce incompatible finite graphs.
- No finite invariant packet predicts the transition target.

## CELL 3 — Complete Fixed-Character Scheme

### Question

What are all irreducible fixed characters, what are their local scheme types, and
how many observables are required to distinguish them?

### Work

- Certify the full numerical solution set with interval methods.
- For every point classify:
  - reduced isolated;
  - nonreduced double;
  - higher multiplicity;
  - positive-dimensional component.
- Compute exact/certified trace and commutator-observable tables.
- Test whether one `kappa` separates all certified points.
- If finite and separated, construct interpolation relations among observables.

### Kill conditions

- An unresolved positive-dimensional irreducible component.
- Failure to certify completeness.
- No small observable set separates the points.

# Campaign stop rule

The campaign ends only when every cell is one of:

- `PROVED/CLOSED`;
- `REFUTED/CLOSED`;
- `BLOCKED BY A NAMED EXTERNAL THEOREM OR COMPUTATIONAL CLASS`;
- `EXPERIMENT-READY`.

“Interesting pattern” is not a terminal state.
