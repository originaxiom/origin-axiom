## The defining ideal and the gauge stratification

Over `K = ℚ(ω)` (`ω²+ω+1=0`), `A = diag(1,1,ω,ω²)`: a bundle rep `(A,t)` satisfies (eliminating `B`) the single matrix
equation `t A⁻² t A = A⁻¹ t A t`, equivalent to `S = tAt` vanishing off the pattern `{(0,0),(0,1),(1,0),(1,1),(2,3),
(3,2)}` — **10 quadratics in the 16 entries of `t`** (ideal `I`; same construction as B89's, lifted to a generic `t`).
The gauge group `Z(A) = GL(V₁)×GL(V_ω)×GL(V_{ω²})` makes the full 16-var primary decomposition intractable, so we
stratify by **gauge-rank of the coupling block `Q = t[0:2,2:4]`** (rank is gauge-invariant). Every `Q` is gauge-normal
to: rank 2 → `I₂`; rank 1 → `[[1,0],[0,0]]`, `[[1,1],[0,0]]`, or `[[0,1],[0,0]]`; rank 0 → `0`. These slices **exhaust**
all reps up to gauge, and each is a tractable fixed-`Q` ideal.

## Methodology note (verify-don't-trust earned its keep)

Three wrong turns were caught before banking:
1. A **symbol-name collision** (generic entries `t12` vs B89's free param `t12`) made the consistency gate fail —
   caught by the gate itself; fixed by naming entries `x{i}{j}`.
2. The **Schur commutant test is NOT a valid irreducibility test** — a reducible *non-semisimple* rep can have
   commutant = scalars (dim 1). A floating-point commutant pass spuriously read the *provably-reducible* `Q=0`
   stratum as "irreducible." The contradiction with the symbolic `Q=0` exhibit exposed the error; the correct test is
   **Burnside** (algebra-dim), computed exactly over F_p and **validated** against the `Q=0` exhibit (all reducible) and
   B89's known irreducibility (rank Q=2 irreducible).
3. A naive word-BFS `algebra_dim` blew up exponentially on reducible reps; replaced by span-closure (≤16 basis elts).

The committed result is the **exhibited** decomposition (`decomposition.json`) + the **exact** F_p classification
(`irreducibility_fp.json`), both re-runnable. No floating-point claim is load-bearing.
