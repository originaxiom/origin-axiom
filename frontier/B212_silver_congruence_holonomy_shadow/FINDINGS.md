## (1) The congruence-group shadow ⟨R,L⟩ mod N = SL(2,ℤ/N)

`⟨R,L⟩ = SL(2,ℤ)`, so its reduction mod the discriminant `N=m²+4` is **all of `SL(2,ℤ/N)`** — a property of the
*modulus*, not the individual `m`:

| m | N=m²+4 | `\|⟨R,L⟩ mod N\|` | group |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 golden | 5 | 120 | `SL(2,𝔽₅)=2I=E₈` (B206) |
| 2 silver | 8 | 384 | `SL(2,ℤ/8)` |
| 3 bronze | 13 | 2184 | `SL(2,𝔽₁₃)` |

This is the "shadow group" of B206 — and it is what the "S₃" label was really about (silver's `⟨R,L⟩ mod 2 =
SL(2,𝔽₂) = S₃`), **not** the silver monodromy.

## (2) The monodromy element is trivial mod 2 for even m — the real correction

The **monodromy element** `RᵐLᵐ = M_m²` reduced mod a prime `p∣m²+4` has order `2Q(m)` generically (B211,
reconfirmed), **but is the identity exactly when `m` is even.** In particular

```
  silver  R²L² ≡ I (mod 2)   — TRIVIAL, not S₃.
```

So B210's "silver → S₃" conflated the `⟨R,L⟩` *group* shadow (1) with the *monodromy element*, which is trivial.
(Contrast golden: `RL mod 5` is a genuine order-10 element of `2I`.)

## (3) The characterization (proved)

```
  RᵐLᵐ ≡ I (mod p)  for a prime p ∣ m²+4   ⇔   p ∣ m   ⇔   p = 2 and m even.
```
Proof: `RᵐLᵐ = M_m² = [[m²+1, m],[m, 1]]`; mod `p` with `m² ≡ −4` this is `[[−3, m],[m, 1]]`, which equals `I` iff
`−3 ≡ 1` (forcing `p∣4 ⇒ p=2`) **and** `m ≡ 0 (mod p)`. And `p∣m` together with `p∣m²+4` forces `p∣4 ⇒ p=2`, i.e.
`m` even. Verified `m=1..15`. So the only metallic mean whose monodromy vanishes mod its own discriminant prime is
the **even-m / prime-2** family; silver is the smallest.

## (4) The hyperbolic-trace-field shadow (the B210 analogue) — silver is degenerate, *computed*

Golden's holonomy reduces mod `(√−3)` to the **full** `SL(2,𝔽₃)=2T=E₆` (B210, integral). For silver (m136), via
`snap.polished_holonomy`:
- The **generators** `a,b,c` are **not ℤ[i]-integral** — `tr(a)≈−1.554−0.644i`, `|tr(a)|²≈2√2` — so the *full*
  trace field strictly contains the invariant field `ℚ(i)` (it carries `√2`, i.e. `≈ℚ(ζ₈)`).
- The **squares** have traces in `ℤ[i]`: `tr(a²)=2i`, `tr(b²)=2`, `tr(c²)=−2i` (so the invariant trace field is

- **All square-traces vanish mod `(1+i)`** (`2=−i(1+i)²≡0`, `2i=(1+i)²≡0`): in the residue field
  `ℤ[i]/(1+i)=𝔽₂` every square reduces to a **trace-0** element of `SL(2,𝔽₂)=S₃` (identity or an involution) —
  **no trace-1 / order-3 element survives.** So the prime-2 hyperbolic shadow carries **no McKay-exceptional
  (order-3) structure** — *degenerate, now computed at the trace level*, in sharp contrast to golden's order-3-rich

- **But** the square-*matrices* are not in `SL(2,ℤ[i])` (integral diagonals, off-diagonals in `ℚ(ζ₈)`): the
  holonomy is a **quaternion order over `ℚ(i)`**. So the literal "image group in `SL(2,𝔽₂)=S₃`" requires the
  *quaternion-order* reduction, **not** naive matrix reduction → the image **group** is a **named residual** (needs
  the integral quaternion model). The banked, computed fact is the **trace-degeneration**.
