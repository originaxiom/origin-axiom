The campaign runs in WAVES, not phases. Each wave's output feeds the
next wave's design.

## STOPPING RULES

**Natural stop (discovery):** A probe reveals that the dark sector
has specific, exact, computable structure that extends the grammar
table with new FORCED rows. The campaign produced new mathematics.
Bank and publish.

**Natural stop (null):** All probes return zero — the dark sector is
topologically present (h⁰ = 1) but quantumly silent and classically
decoupled. The framework predicts dark matter EXISTS but doesn't
INTERACT. Bank the null honestly.

**Natural stop (wall):** A probe reveals an obstruction that makes
further dark sector computation impossible at this level (e.g., the
V₀ block is too degenerate to carry interesting structure). Bank the
wall. Add it to the Law Map.

**Forced stop (scope creep):** If a probe leads to a SM-facing
comparison that wasn't pre-authorized, STOP and report to the director.
Do not run the comparison. The stopping rule from the main campaign
applies.

**No time limit.** The campaign runs until a natural or forced stop.
Waves can be separated by days or weeks. Each wave's output is banked
independently. The campaign's state is always recoverable from the
banked results.

REDIRECT NOTE: "given what we found, should the next wave change?"

targets are amended. If not: the original plan continues.

choosing a different computational method, adding a control, extending
a level scan) without director approval. STRATEGIC changes (adding a
new target, dropping a target, changing the interpretation framework)
require the redirect note and director approval.

The campaign is a CONVERSATION between the seats and the object. The
object's answers shape the questions. The questions are not fixed in
advance. The discipline is in the sealing, the controls, and the
gating — not in the rigidity of the plan.
