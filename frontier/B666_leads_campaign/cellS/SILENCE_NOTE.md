## 1. The gluing statement

**Proposition (no canonical unit section).** A matter sector that is
scale-rigid in the S′ sense — all outputs algebraic numbers structured
by finite/profinite symmetry, Hom(G, ℝ₊) = 0, every equivariant map
into a nontrivial scale representation zero — provides **no canonical
section of the ℝ₊ unit torsor**: by S′ §3 there is no nonzero
G-equivariant assignment of a dimensionful quantity, in particular no
preferred energy density, from the sector's own data. Any unit section
must therefore come from OUTSIDE the sector (a boundary/global
choice), not from its local structure. ∎ (This is S′ re-read as a
statement about sections rather than predictions.)

**Consequence for the gravitational coupling.** Couple such a sector
to gravity and ask where the cosmological term Λ — a dimensionful
quantity, weight ≠ 0 on the unit torsor — can live. The local theory
of the sector cannot supply it (the Proposition). The two classical
formulations that make this structural fact manifest are theorems of
the literature, not of this repo:

- **Trace-free / unimodular route.** Replace the Einstein equations by
  their trace-free part, R_μν − ¼g_μν R = 8πG(T_μν − ¼g_μν T), and
  assume energy-momentum conservation ∇^μT_μν = 0 independently. The
  second Bianchi identity then forces ∂_μ(R + 8πG T) = 0, i.e.
  R + 8πG T = 4Λ for some constant Λ, and the full Einstein equations
  with cosmological constant are recovered — **with Λ entering as an
  integration constant** of the field equations, decoupled from any
  vacuum-energy content of the matter sector. The trace-free equations
  go back to Einstein (1919) [3]; the modern viability statement is
  Ellis–van Elst–Murugan–Uzan [2], whose abstract states the theory
  "is indeed a viable theory that resolves the problem of the
  discrepancy between the vacuum energy density and the observed value
  of the cosmological constant," while — quoted with equal weight —
  it "does not solve the fundamental issue of why the cosmological
  constant has the value that is observed cosmologically."
- **Henneaux–Teitelboim route.** In the covariant formulation [1], the
  determinant of the metric is treated as an external field / the
  volume is fixed, and covariance is restored by auxiliary gauge
  fields (a 3-form) with a single zero mode: Λ appears as a constant
  of integration, canonically **conjugate to a global "cosmic time"
  variable** (the spacetime volume mode). Λ is one global degree of
  freedom — a boundary/global mode, not a local field of the matter
  sector.

Both formulations say the same thing in the language S′ speaks:
**Λ is a coordinate on a boundary torsor** (the global mode conjugate
to total 4-volume; equivalently the integration constant fixed only by
a boundary condition), not an output of the local scale-rigid theory.

## 2. The conclusion, at theorem grade

**Theorem (the silence theorem, for scale-rigid frameworks).** Let a
matter sector satisfy the S′ hypotheses. Then in its gravitational
coupling, in the unimodular/trace-free and Henneaux–Teitelboim
formulations, Λ is an integration constant / global conjugate mode,
and NO computation internal to the sector determines it: a determination
would be a nonzero equivariant map from the sector's output algebra
into a nontrivial scale representation, which is zero by S′ §3.
Hence, for such frameworks, **"predict Λ" is a category error with a
proof**: Λ does not live in the framework's output type at all; it
lives on the unit/boundary torsor, where the framework provably has no
canonical point to offer. The correct question a scale-rigid framework
CAN be asked is dimensionless and structural (which finite structures,
which phases, which ratios of like quantities at declared scheme) —
never "what is Λ". ∎ (Scope: a theorem about formulations + the S′
output type; the empirical 60–120-orders-of-magnitude discrepancy [2]
is cited only as the problem's standard statement, nowhere engaged as
a value.)
