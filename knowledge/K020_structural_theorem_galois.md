## The headline

Across every face examined in this era, one verdict recurs: **the object forces *form/structure* — never physical
*values*.** It is selective for its own structure (E₆, the cascade, `κ`, both arithmetic ends, the exponents) and a
flat catalogue for the actual Standard-Model values (couplings, the level, the scale). This is the **structural
theorem**, and it now has a mechanism: **every discrete invariant of the object is a Galois orbit of the object's own
arithmetic Galois group**, so the discreteness is always a *labeling*, never a forced value. *The value-free monad is a
Galois theorem.*

## 1. The two-ended object (B248/B249/B258/B261)

The figure-eight is a **two-ended** arithmetic object. One canonical structure sits at each of two arithmetic poles,
forced apart by Niven's theorem (a rational angle with rational cosine is `0,±1,±½`):

- **Eisenstein end** — `ℚ(√−3)`, the hyperbolic trace field, the cube root `ω`, the group **E₆**.

- **E₇ is excluded** (its field `ℚ(√2)` fails the disc≡1 mod 4 / Niven gate; B256: E₇ is the *silver* m=2 group, not the
  figure-eight's). The two ends are two regimes of one AJ recursion (B261).

This double arithmetic is the program's deepest *object-specific* fact: the figure-eight is the unique minimal object
carrying **both** ends, and most later results reduce to it.
