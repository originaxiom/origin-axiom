# P004 — Expansion is interaction-born

> **Philosophy — motivation only** (`GOVERNANCE.md`). Not a claim; never a premise. Cites the mathematics one-way.
> This is an INTERPRETATION resting on a theorem (the group fact below); read the *reading* as a position, the
> *theorem* as load-bearing.

## The stance

The engine of the entire program is **expansion** — the metallic unit `M_m` is hyperbolic (`|λ| ≠ 1`), and that
hyperbolicity is what makes the trace map a dynamical system with a tower at all. The position taken here is that
expansion is **not a primitive**. It does not exist as a thing the unit *has*; it is **born from an interaction**
of two non-expanding motions.

## The theorem this rests on (load-bearing)

In the mapping-class group the metallic unit factors exactly as
```
   M_m = (twist)^m · (swap),     [[m,1],[1,0]] = [[1,m],[0,1]] · [[0,1],[1,0]].
```
Neither factor expands: the **twist** `[[1,1],[0,1]]` is parabolic (`|λ| = 1`), the **swap** `[[0,1],[1,0]]` has
order 2 (`|λ| = 1`). Their **product is hyperbolic** (`M_1` has eigenvalues `φ, −1/φ`). Stronger and more general:
`SL(2,ℤ)` is generated by **finite-order** (hence non-expanding) elements — `S` of order 4 and `ST` of order 6 —
and `GL(2,ℤ)` adds only the order-2 swap. So **every** tower-bearing (hyperbolic) element is necessarily a product
of non-expanding ones: expansion *cannot* appear as a generator. It is emergent by the structure of the group, not
by choice. (Verified in B120; the factorization and the orders are elementary.)

## The reading

- The unit is a **minimal interaction**, not an atom. `m=0` (swap alone) is the static `|λ|=1` ground state; `m=1`
  (the golden mean) is the threshold where the twist-and-swap interaction first ignites expansion; larger `m`
  turns it up. "Something that grows" is the *composite*, never the part.
- The two ingredients are exactly the two that determine everything downstream (`K008`, B120): **twist-count →
  trace** (the rate) and **swap-count mod 2 → det** (the parity sector, the proved `det=−1` signature, B93/B94).
  So the same interaction that *creates* the expansion also *fixes* the whole tower.
- This is why "the interaction determines the tower" is not a slogan but a structural fact: the tower is a function
  of `(n; trace, det)` and the seed `(trace, det)` is precisely the interaction-word read off `M_m`.

## A correction this entry also records (downgrade, not a kill)

An earlier reading framed the unit as having an *interior* (bulk, order-2) and a *boundary* (cusp, the order-4
scalar `c`) that it cannot close from inside — a "Markov-blanket / boundary-open" picture, with degree=rank as the
interior↔boundary coupling. **That reading is a low-rank `n∈{3,4}` phenomenon, not an all-`n` property.** B117 shows
the tower is one internally-generated object (the Sym two-sequence) organized by an *internal* order-2 inversion
involution (the contragredient; B118's `(−1)^{h+1}` sign rule, itself a `det=−1` feature), with **no all-`n`
boundary the unit fails to close**; and B120/Q2 shows the boundary datum `c` and the geometric degree=rank live
exactly where the irreducible principal rep does — `n∈{3,4}` only. So the "needs to reach beyond itself" reading is
**downgraded to `n∈{3,4}`** (real there, not general) — recorded, per `P003`, as a boundary discovered. What
**survives at all `n`** is the present entry: expansion is interaction-born.
