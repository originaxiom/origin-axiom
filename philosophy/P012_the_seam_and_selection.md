## The provocation, twice answered
`P011` answered the owner's "our framework is dynamical, why need dynamics externally?" by separating *generative*
(internal) from *actualizing* (external) dynamics, and gave **Curie's principle** as the reason a symmetric object
cannot self-select. Two corrections followed, each from a genuine push:

1. **The wall is at the CLOSURE, not in the object** (`B286`). The figure-eight is **open** — a complement, with a
   cusp = its interface with "the nothing." Curie is a *closed*-system theorem; an open object breaks its symmetries
   through **boundary conditions**, and **closing it (Dehn filling) *is* the symmetry-breaking** — constitutive, not
   foreign (a complement is *defined* by what it excludes).
2. **The wall is a GAUGE/external wall, not a Curie wall** (`B295`, Chat-2). Curie has a famous loophole —
   **spontaneous symmetry breaking** under a running control parameter — so "Curie forbids the sign" was the wrong
   argument. But the positive replacement is *not* established either: the SSB potential is **absent** (the program's
   $V(\tau)$ is the modular-$\tau$/golden/cubic object, disjoint from the Eisenstein $\pm\pi/6$ vacua), and "$\tau$ is
   gauged" is an **unverified** stop-gate. So the sign is external because the object is **CP-symmetric**, and the
   *mechanism* (SSB or gauge-fixing) is genuinely **open**.
