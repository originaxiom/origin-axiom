## The move

"Why is there something rather than nothing?" is malformed: to *define* "nothing" is to determine it — to make it
*something*. The only well-posed direction is the **negative** one: *"what is not-nothing?"* And its minimal
answers do not single out one object — they form a **self-generating family**. A self-referential foundation that
produced a *unique* seed would be the suspicious outcome; fixed-point phenomena generically give *sets*, not
points. So **"we end up with a family" is the intended shape, not a gap to be closed** (see `METALLIC_FOUNDATIONS.md`).

## The four chosen premises (and the honest part)

The mathematics this motivates rests on **four chosen premises** — they are *premises*, openly chosen, not
theorems:

1. **Resist the trivial maximally.** The structure that least collapses back to "nothing" is the **most
   irrational** rotation — `φ`, the golden mean, by **Hurwitz's theorem** (the worst-approximable number).
2. **Self-reference, not external input.** The object generates itself (a once-punctured-torus monodromy / `F₂`
   trace map), with no datum imported from outside.
3. **A family, `m` free.** The not-nothing condition cuts out the **metallic family** `M_m=[[m,1],[1,0]]` up to
   `GL(2,ℤ)` conjugacy — the `det=−1` / period-1 slice (B92, *proved*) — not a single member.
4. **The member is contingent.** Selecting `m=1` (golden) requires importing a **metric**: `m=1` is the *systole*
   (shortest closed geodesic on the modular surface). The family is determined; the member is a choice.

**The irreducibility of choice (the honest position).** Given these four premises, the family follows
*necessarily* — but the premises themselves are **chosen**. The choice is not eliminated; it is **relocated and
made explicit** (premise 4 is the relocation made visible). Any attempt to force a single seed without a metric
hides the choice rather than removing it. *This is the position P002 is careful never to overstate.*
