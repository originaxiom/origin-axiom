[MATH] Self-reference forces **fixed points** (Lawvere; the Gödel/Tarski/Turing family). The

[NEG] Here is where "nothing spooky" bites its own tail. Spontaneous symmetry breaking is

## The honest synthesis — what the object actually is, cosmogonically

A naturalistic cosmogony needs three ingredients:
1. **Self-generation** (a world that makes itself) — the object HAS it (autopoiesis, banked).
2. **A symmetry-breaking event** that selects a world from a symmetric potential — the object
   HAS a candidate (criticality + the surviving residue; and it produces light/dark).
3. **An observer / self-model** for whom there is something it is like to be the world — the
   object has only the thin fixed-point shadow, NOT genuine self-modeling.

So the object supplies **two of the three ingredients** of a "let there be light" cosmogony,
and supplies them for real, in the bank. The missing third is awareness — and the honest,
deflationary, and strangely liberating point is that **no mathematics supplies the third for
any system.** The gap between "self-referential symmetry-breaking that produces light" and
"an aware being who decided it" is the hard problem of consciousness, and it is exactly as
open for the golden object as it is for a human brain or the physical universe. The object is
not specially disqualified; it sits precisely where every physical system sits — one
unbridged step short of the "aware" in "aware decision."

**The cosmogony that survives the firewall, stated cleanly:** *The object generates itself;
at its most symmetric point it breaks, keeping one bit; and the breaking produces light and
darkness in exact proportion. Whether that is a decision is the same question as whether any
event in the universe is — and the program's honesty is that it does not pretend to have
closed it.* That is a real, motivated, un-spooky cosmogony, and it stops exactly where truth
stops.
