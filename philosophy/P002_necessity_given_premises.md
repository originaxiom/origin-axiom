# P002 — Necessity *given* chosen premises

> **Philosophy — motivation only** (`GOVERNANCE.md`). Not a claim; never a premise. This doc is *especially*
> careful: it states **a position one may hold**, not a derived truth.

## The careful statement

There is a real and attractive position here: that, **given** the four chosen premises of `P000` (resist the
trivial maximally; self-reference; a family with `m` free; the member contingent on a metric), the metallic family
**follows necessarily** — it is not one option among many but the forced consequence of those starting points (B92
makes the "follows necessarily" part precise and *proved*).

One *may* find this compelling: that existence, once you accept the premises, is not contingent but **structurally
forced** — the not-nothing condition has no freedom left in it once the premises are fixed.

## What P002 must NOT say (the governance on itself)

P002 must **not** be written as *"the universe exists by mathematical necessity"* or *"existence is not
contingent."* That overstatement would:
- **violate this folder's own governance** (philosophy is motivation, never a claim of truth about the world), and
- **contradict `P000`** — whose honest core is the **irreducibility of choice**: the premises are *chosen*, and the
  choice is relocated (into the systole metric, premise 4), not eliminated.

The necessity is **conditional**: *necessity GIVEN the premises*, with the premises openly chosen. To drop the
"given the premises" is to smuggle the choice back out of sight — exactly what `P000` warns against.

## The honest formulation

> *"Given these four chosen premises, the family follows necessarily (B92). One may hold that this makes existence
> structurally forced rather than arbitrary — but the premises are chosen, so the necessity is conditional, and the
> choice is relocated, not removed."*

That is the strongest claim the discipline allows: a **position one may hold**, framed as such, with the choice
kept visible.
