# 01 — The seed object

> Narrative, not claim. Cites the mathematics one-way.

The question of chapter 00 was handed to the simplest object that could carry it: the free group on two
generators `F₂ = ⟨a,b⟩` — the fundamental group of the **once-punctured torus**, the simplest surface that is
neither a sphere nor a plane. Its mapping-class group `GL(2,ℤ)` acts on the space of representations by a
polynomial **trace map** on the Fricke coordinates `(tr A, tr B, tr AB)`.

The "resist the trivial maximally" commitment became a precise classification (**B92**, V76, `proven`): among the
non-negative hyperbolic unimodular `2×2` matrices, the dominant eigenvalue has a purely-periodic period-1
continued fraction **iff `det = −1`** — which cuts out the **metallic family** `M_m = [[m,1],[1,0]]`, with `m`
free, up to `GL(2,ℤ)` conjugacy. The family is *determined*; the member is *contingent* — selecting `m=1` (the
golden seed) requires a metric, and `m=1` is the **systole** (the shortest closed geodesic on the modular
surface).

That `det = −1` is not an aesthetic choice was the next link (**B93/B94**): `det = −1` is exactly what gives the
later tower its parity — *"universal catalog, det = −1 parity."* The honest part, banked from the start: the four
naive premises admit `det = +1` too, so `det = −1` is an *operative extra condition*, not something derivable from
"simplest" without importing a metric. The choice was relocated and made visible, not eliminated
(`../philosophy/P000`, `../philosophy/P002`).

The seed object was now fixed: a self-generating family of trace maps, with one distinguished-by-a-metric member.
The question was: what does perturbing it produce?
