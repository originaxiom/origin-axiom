# 10 — Chirality, and completeness

> Narrative, not claim. Cites the mathematics one-way.

After the object crystallized as a representation (chapter 09), two questions that had been circling for a long time
finally closed — both *internal*, both about the object itself rather than about physics. One was the chirality axis
(the project's one *proven* obstruction to Standard-Model-like structure). The other was a precise loose end in the
degree=rank story.

## The chirality axis closes

The firewall's one proven obstruction to physics had always been **chirality** — parity violation. The question was
sharp: can the construction *prefer* a handedness, or is any chirality a convention its own `R↔L` mirror symmetry
undoes? The arc **B144–B147** answered it completely.

- **B144** (interactions): for cusp-glued composites of metallic pieces the family is **mirror-closed** — the mirror
  of a composite is another composite of the same pieces — so seed-heterogeneity injects *contingency* (a discrete
  `κ`-fork, B131) but never a *preferred handedness*. The firewall extends from single objects to interactions.
- **B145–B146** (can chirality be forced?): chirality is **forced and generic** — most hyperbolic once-punctured-torus
  bundles are chiral — but it is always either **self-mirror** (the canonical singles) or **mirror-paired** (the
  composites). No *single canonical* object is chiral: the figure-eight is the amphichiral minimum, and chirality first
  appears strictly above it. The sharp statement is the **preferred-handedness dichotomy**: `M` and its mirror agree on
  every orientation-*independent* invariant, so no canonical (orientation-independent) selection can ever prefer a
  handedness — the `κ`-fork is genuine selection because `κ` is orientation-independent; a chirality-fork is always
  convention because handedness is orientation-sensitive.
- **B147** (the load-bearing correction): B145's *arithmeticity* arm — "no arithmetic chiral bundle" — was refuted
  outright. The chiral mirror pair `RRL/RLL` is **fully arithmetic** (invariant trace field `ℚ(√−7)`, integral traces,
  confirmed by a Humbert volume cross-check). So "arithmetic ⟹ amphichiral" is simply false; the surviving statement
  rests on the volume-minimality / palindromic-period arms and the dichotomy, not on arithmeticity. The catch was found
  the disciplined way — a result that contradicted a cited "exactly two" was held until two independent methods agreed
  and a real bug (reading a leading coefficient at the wrong degree) was fixed.

The reading this licenses (`../knowledge/K017`): chirality is **contingent relative to the metallic minimality
criteria**, not forced — and no canonical principle prefers a handedness. The chirality axis is closed.

## Degree=rank, complete on the irreducible locus

B89 had proved `M⁴ = L` symbolic-exact over `ℚ(ω)` on an *explicit, posited* family of `SL(4)` figure-eight bundle
reps. A precise loose end remained: was that family the *whole* story, or only a checked corner? **B149** settled it.

The defining ideal of the bundle reps (for the principal `A`-spectrum `{1,1,ω,ω²}`) was decomposed — gauge-stratified
by the rank of the coupling block, the only way past the gauge symmetry — and the components exhibited symbolically.
Then irreducibility was classified **exactly over a finite field** (the Burnside test: the generated algebra is the
full matrix algebra iff the rep is absolutely irreducible). The result: the **only** stratum carrying irreducible reps
is B89's family; every other component is reducible or vacuous (`det t = 0`). So B89's family is the **complete
component of irreducible reps**, and `M⁴ = L` is **unconditional on the irreducible locus** — the scope upgrades from
"on the principal component" to "the principal component *is* the irreducible locus." B89's proof is untouched; its
reach is now known exactly.

This stage is also where the discipline earned its keep most visibly: the irreducibility verdict flipped twice under
scrutiny before settling, because a tempting test (the Schur commutant) is *invalid* for non-semisimple reps — it
falsely flagged a provably-reducible stratum as irreducible. The correct test (Burnside, over `F_p`) was *validated
against* a stratum already proven reducible by hand before it was trusted. The banked result rests on the validated
test, not the convenient one.

## Where this leaves the object

Two internal questions that had been open for the project's whole middle are now closed: chirality (contingent, never
preferred) and the completeness of degree=rank (the family is the whole irreducible locus). Neither touched physics —
both are facts about the object. With its internal structure this fully mapped, the object was ready for the one
outward question that remained: *what is its symmetry, really?* — and whether that reaches across to physics.
