# 03 — Degree=rank and the knot invariants

> Narrative, not claim. Cites the mathematics one-way.

The trace map turned out to know about knots. The once-punctured-torus bundle whose monodromy is `M²` is exactly
the **figure-eight knot** complement, and the fixed locus of the squared trace map *is* the A-polynomial curve.
Computing it reproduced the published **Cooper–Long (1996)** figure-eight A-polynomial **exactly** — the first
derivation of that invariant from a trace-map computation (**B67**, V20). The SL(3) version followed: the fixed
locus decomposed into exactly three components matching the Falbel et al. Dehn-filling A-variety, with the clean
relations `M³ = L` and `M³L = 1` holding to 50 digits (**B71**, V43–V48).

Alongside ran a second exact thread, **degree=rank**: on the figure-eight character variety the longitude is a
*power* of the meridian, `[A,B] = (−1)^{n−1} μⁿ`, proved at `n = 3, 4` (**B71/B89**), with the A-polynomial
family `L = (−1)^{n−1} Mⁿ` (**B83**). The SL(4) case `M⁴ = L` was nailed symbolic-exact over `ℚ(ω)` (**B89**,
V72) — and, importantly, the honest scope was banked with it: the generic gauge-slice branch is vacuous; the real
representations live on a rank-drop locus, a trap that was flagged so later work would avoid it.

Two clean, independent deep facts — a knot invariant and a power law — both anchored to the same little family of
trace maps. The natural next question was whether they were *one* object.
