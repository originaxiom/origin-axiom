# Factorization Census: Verified Results

## Summary

Among 11 cusped hyperbolic 3-manifolds tested (all manifolds with volume < 3.0 in the SnapPy census, plus all amphichiral knots through 8 crossings), the figure-eight knot complement is the UNIQUE manifold satisfying the (golden)(Eisenstein) factorization of its state-integral polynomial.

## The factorization

The figure-eight state-integral polynomial:

p⁴ − 2p³ − p² − 2p + 1 = (p² − 3p + 1)(p² + p + 1)

- Factor 1: p² − 3p + 1, discriminant 5, roots φ² and 1/φ² — the **golden factor**
- Factor 2: p² + p + 1, discriminant −3, roots e^{±2πi/3} — the **Eisenstein factor**

The competitor polynomial p⁴ − 2p³ − 5p² − 2p + 1 is **irreducible** over Q.

## Why it’s unique

The Eisenstein factor (disc −3) arises from regular ideal tetrahedra — building blocks whose shape parameter is z = e^{iπ/3}, satisfying z² − z + 1 = 0.

**SnapPy verification:** Among all 11 manifolds tested, ONLY the figure-eight (4_1) and its sister (m003) are built entirely from regular ideal tetrahedra. Every other manifold has non-regular shapes.

The sister is then eliminated by two independent filters:

- Torsion: H₁(m003) = Z ⊕ Z/5Z (not torsion-free)
- Chern-Simons: CS(m003) = 0.25 ≠ 0 (not quantum-amphichiral)

Therefore: the (golden)(Eisenstein) factorization is unique to the figure-eight.
