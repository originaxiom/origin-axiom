## 9. The \(F\)-symbol audit

The nontrivial Fibonacci \(F\)-matrix can be written as

\[
F=
\begin{pmatrix}
\varphi^{-1}&\varphi^{-1/2}\\
\varphi^{-1/2}&-\varphi^{-1}
\end{pmatrix}.
\]

It is a real unitary/orthogonal associativity matrix with a negative entry. It is not an integer positive transfer matrix.

The direct identification

```text
record gluing = Fibonacci F-move
```

fails.

The record matrices \(L,R,A\) are shears or positive hyperbolic count matrices. They are not \(F\)-symbols.

However, the squared probability matrix is

\[
|F|^2=
\begin{pmatrix}
\varphi^{-2}&\varphi^{-1}\\
\varphi^{-1}&\varphi^{-2}
\end{pmatrix}.
\]

This matrix is doubly stochastic and has stay/switch probabilities

\[
P(\mathrm{stay})=\varphi^{-2},
\qquad
P(\mathrm{switch})=\varphi^{-1}.
\]

The odds ratio is

\[
\frac{P(\mathrm{switch})}{P(\mathrm{stay})}=\varphi.
\]

The Perron eigenvector of \(A\) is proportional to

\[
(\varphi,1).
\]

Therefore, if one uses the ordered Perron components as stay/switch weights,

\[
\mathrm{stay}:\mathrm{switch}=1:\varphi,
\]

then normalization gives

\[
P(\mathrm{stay})=\frac1{1+\varphi}=\varphi^{-2},
\]

\[
P(\mathrm{switch})=\frac{\varphi}{1+\varphi}=\varphi^{-1}.
\]

Thus \(|F|^2\) can be reconstructed conditionally from \(A\)'s Perron eigenvector ratio.

The key word is conditionally. Plain maximum entropy gives \(1/2,1/2\). Detailed balance leaves a free parameter. To obtain \(|F|^2\), one must add a Perron-degeneracy or Perron-switch principle. This is a meaningful bridge rule, but not yet a first-principles derivation.
