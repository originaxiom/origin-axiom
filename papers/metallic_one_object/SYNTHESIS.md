## 1. The one object

Let $\Sigma_{1,1}$ be the once-punctured torus, $\pi_1=\langle A,B\rangle$ free of rank 2. Its mapping class
group is $\mathrm{MCG}(\Sigma_{1,1})\cong \mathrm{SL}(2,\mathbb{Z})$, generated by the two Dehn twists
$R=\left[\begin{smallmatrix}1&1\\0&1\end{smallmatrix}\right]$,
$L=\left[\begin{smallmatrix}1&0\\1&1\end{smallmatrix}\right]$. The **metallic family** is the sequence of
mapping classes
$$\varphi_m \;=\; R^m L^m \;=\; \left[\begin{smallmatrix}1+m^2 & m\\ m & 1\end{smallmatrix}\right],
\qquad \operatorname{tr}\varphi_m = m^2+2,$$
hyperbolic for all $m\ge 1$, with larger eigenvalue $\lambda_m^2$ where
$$\boxed{\;\lambda_m=\tfrac{m+\sqrt{m^2+4}}{2}\;}\quad(\text{the }m\text{-th metallic mean};\ \lambda_1=\varphi\text{ golden},\ \lambda_2=1+\sqrt2\text{ silver}).$$
$\lambda_m$ is the fundamental unit (norm $-1$) of the real quadratic field $\mathbb{Q}(\sqrt{m^2+4})$, with the
period-1 continued fraction $\lambda_m=[m;m,m,\dots]$ **[banked K002; verified this session]**. The $m=1$
member is the **figure-eight knot complement** (its once-punctured-torus bundle has monodromy
$RL=\left[\begin{smallmatrix}2&1\\1&1\end{smallmatrix}\right]$).

The single dynamical system underlying everything is the **trace map** — the $\mathrm{SL}(2,\mathbb{Z})$ action
on the Fricke character variety $(x,y,z)=(\operatorname{tr}A,\operatorname{tr}B,\operatorname{tr}AB)$ by the
Dehn twists $\tau_R,\tau_L$, preserving the **Fricke commutator invariant**
$$\kappa \;=\; \operatorname{tr}[A,B] \;=\; x^2+y^2+z^2-xyz-2 .$$

## 2. Face I — algebraic geometry: the character variety

The metallic monodromy **is** the $\mathrm{SL}(2,\mathbb{Z})$ MCG action on the Fricke cubic; the Dehn twists
preserve $\kappa$; $\operatorname{tr}\varphi_m=m^2+2$; the larger eigenvalue is $\lambda_m^2$; the $\kappa=-2$
slice is the Markov surface **[banked B148/V137]**.

**The $\mathrm{SL}(n)$ tower is this same face at higher rank.** The figure-eight $\mathrm{SL}(3)$ character
variety = 3 dim-2 components (geometric $=\mathrm{Sym}^2$ + two Dehn-filling), the silver = 4
**[banked B71, B201–B203]**, all from the trace-map fixed-locus structure. The metallic family lifts to
$\mathrm{SL}(n)$ monodromy reps with the figure-eight $A$-polynomial family $L=(-1)^{n-1}M^n$; the
"degree=rank" reading (A-polynomial slope $=$ rank) is the $\mathrm{SL}(n)$ generalization of the trace map's
fixed locus **[banked B89–B95, B153]** — but it is **refuted at $n\ge5$** (SL(5): $k=2\ne5$), and the true
exponent is **order-determined, not rank-determined**, valued as the metallic $A$-polynomial slope read on the
geometric (cusped, infinite-order-meridian) component; no closed-form $k(o,m)$ survives **[banked B198–B199]**.
So B67/B71 (figure-eight as trace-map fixed locus) and the SL(n) tower are **one object at different ranks**.
Owner of the general theory: Fricke–Vogt; Goldman; Cantat–Loray; Cooper–Long; Heusener–Muñoz–Porti
**[literature]**.

## 3. Face II — hyperbolic geometry: a closed geodesic

$\varphi_m\in\mathrm{SL}(2,\mathbb{Z})$ is a hyperbolic conjugacy class, i.e. a **closed geodesic on the
modular surface** $\mathbb{H}/\mathrm{SL}(2,\mathbb{Z})$, of length $=2\log(\text{larger eigenvalue})$:
$$\ell(\varphi_m)=2\log\lambda_m^2=4\log\lambda_m \qquad(\text{the generator } M_m=\left[\begin{smallmatrix}m&1\\1&0\end{smallmatrix}\right] \text{ gives the systole } 2\log\lambda_m,\ m{=}1\text{ shortest}).$$
The geodesic **multiplier** is $\lambda_m^2$, which is **exactly** the Cantat–Loray **dynamical degree** (=
topological entropy) of the mapping class acting on the character variety **[banked B150/V139; verified this
session: $\mathrm{eig}(M_m^2)=\lambda_m^2$ exactly]**. So the length spectrum of the metallic family is
$\{4\log\lambda_m\}=2\times$ the **regulators** of $\mathbb{Q}(\sqrt{m^2+4})$. Owner: classical hyperbolic
geometry; Cantat–Loray **[literature]**.

## 4. Face III — spectral theory: a quasicrystal

## 5. Face IV — quantum topology / quantum algebra

The **same** $\mathrm{SL}(2,\mathbb{Z})$ acts on the **quantized** character variety, in two regimes:
- **$q$ = root of unity → WRT.** $Z_k=\operatorname{tr}\rho_k(\varphi_m)$ in the $\mathrm{SU}(2)_k$ modular
  representation is the Witten–Reshetikhin–Turaev invariant of the (closed-torus) mapping torus. We proved its
  level-magnitude is exactly periodic, $\operatorname{per}|Z_k|=\operatorname{lcm}(a,b)(4+ab)/\gcd(4+ab,4)$,
  via Gauss-sum reciprocity, and verified by direct reading that this **is Jeffrey (1992)'s invariant** (her
  eq 4.8 carries exactly the moduli $\operatorname{tr}\mp2=ab,\,4{+}ab=\det(\gamma{\pm}I)$); the period is a
  corollary of her formula **[banked B204/V197–V200; periodicity proved; framework = Jeffrey, literature]**.
- **$q$ generic → skein / DAHA.** The Kauffman skein algebra of $\Sigma_{1,1}$ (central element $\Omega(q)$,
  classical limit $\kappa$) carries the quantum Dehn twists $R_q,L_q$ as automorphisms (classical limit the
  Kohmoto map), with q-Chebyshev $[m]_q$ structure **[banked B205/V201]**. This algebra is the Askey–Wilson
  algebra / spherical DAHA; the q-deformed metallic means are Morier-Genoud–Ovsienko's q-reals. Owner: Jeffrey;
  Bullock–Przytycki; Terwilliger; Cherednik; Morier-Genoud–Ovsienko **[literature]**.

Three independent specialnesses of the golden mean coincide, all forced by $5$: **dynamically extremal** (the
most badly-approximable number, smallest regulator, the systole), **arithmetically exceptional** ($2I=E_8$),
and **least-hierarchical** (smallest volume $2v_{\mathrm{tet}}$, the floor of the bounded volume window
$[2v_{\mathrm{tet}}, 2v_{\mathrm{oct}})$, `[B207]`). The same "$5$" runs through the period law too: the WRT
level-period $P(m)$ is always a multiple of the squarefree part of the discriminant $m^2+4$, and at golden that
