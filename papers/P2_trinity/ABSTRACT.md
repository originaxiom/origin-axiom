# Paper 2 — abstract (polished)

The figure-eight knot 4₁ is distinguished twice over: it is the mapping torus of the simplest
golden cat map (forcing the field ℚ(√5) through its dynamics) and the unique arithmetic knot
(Reid, 1991; forcing ℚ(√−3) through its hyperbolic geometry). These two uniquenesses about a
single knot compose — √5·√−3 = √−15 — into the field ℚ(√5,√−3), the Hilbert class field of
ℚ(√−15). We show that this double uniqueness is the organizing principle behind a complete
family of invariants of 4₁, exhibiting the same object in seven mathematical languages: its
symbolic coding (a Sturmian system), its dynamics (Anosov, entropy 4 log φ), its Reidemeister
torsion in the exceptional adjoint representation 𝔢₆, its quantum invariant (Kashaev, in the
volume-conjecture regime), its quantized value measure, its L-function landscape, and its
theta value theory.

The central identity binding these faces is that the E₆ adjoint torsion equals the golden cat
map's periodic-orbit product — precisely, 5F₂ⱼ² = |det(A²ʲ − I)| = #Fix(A²ʲ) — so that
"quantum topology" and "dynamics" are literally the same computation; both the torsion and
its Hessian spectrum are golden, with prime content exactly the Fibonacci apparition primes.
We give the explicit dictionaries between all seven faces and show that the object's
apparent "walls" — that it produces no dimensionful scale and no canonical mixing frame — are
not obstructions but the correct properties of golden-field arithmetic: a number is
dimensionless, and the genus characters of a field of class number two are independent. The
figure-eight thus emerges as the unique minimal geometric realization of the arithmetic of
the golden × Eisenstein compositum — a complete, self-contained instance of the trinity
between quantum topology, arithmetic, and dynamics.
