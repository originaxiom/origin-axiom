# Paper 2 — abstract (polished)

The figure-eight knot 4₁ is distinguished twice over: it is the mapping torus of the simplest
golden cat map (forcing the field ℚ(√5) through its dynamics) and the unique arithmetic knot
(Reid, 1991; forcing ℚ(√−3) through its hyperbolic geometry). These two uniquenesses about a
single knot compose — √5·√−3 = √−15 — into the field ℚ(√5,√−3), the Hilbert class field of
ℚ(√−15). We show that this double uniqueness is the organizing principle behind a complete
family of invariants of 4₁, exhibiting the same object in several mathematical languages: its
symbolic coding (a Sturmian system), its dynamics (Anosov, entropy 4 log φ), its two
Reidemeister-type torsions in the exceptional adjoint representation 𝔢₆ (the dynamical zeta of
the monodromy and the geometric torsion of the holonomy), its quantum invariant (Kashaev, in
the volume-conjecture regime), its quantized value measure, its L-function landscape, and its
theta value theory.

The central identity binding these faces is that the object carries TWO 𝔢₆ torsions, one per
uniqueness. The dynamical zeta of the golden monodromy A = [[2,1],[1,1]] equals the cat map's
periodic-orbit product — precisely, 5F₂ⱼ² = |det(A²ʲ − I)| = #Fix(A²ʲ) — so that this
"quantum-topological" invariant and the dynamics are literally the same golden computation,
with prime content exactly the Fibonacci apparition primes. The geometric Reidemeister torsion
at the discrete-faithful holonomy ρ_geo (whose entries carry the Eisenstein unit ω), by
contrast, is Eisenstein: its twisted Alexander coefficients are rational (√−3 cancels, Galois-
invariant at every exponent) and its canonical adjoint value is −3 = disc ℚ(√−3) — Porti's
adjoint torsion form (independently confirmed three ways). The two torsions realize the double
uniqueness concretely — √5 (dynamics) and √−3 (geometry) as two computed discriminants meeting
at √5·√−3 = √−15. We give the explicit dictionaries between all these faces and show that the object's
apparent "walls" — that it produces no dimensionful scale and no canonical mixing frame — are
not obstructions but the correct properties of golden-field arithmetic: a number is
dimensionless, and the genus characters of a field of class number two are independent. The
figure-eight thus emerges as the unique minimal geometric realization of the arithmetic of
the golden × Eisenstein compositum — a complete, self-contained instance of the trinity
between quantum topology, arithmetic, and dynamics.
