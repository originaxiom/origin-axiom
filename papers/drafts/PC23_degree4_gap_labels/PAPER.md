# A Quartic-Pisot Substitution Chain: the First Degree-Four Gap-Label Module, with a Four-Coupling Photonic Test

**Authors:** originaxiom

**Status labels used throughout:** [PROVED] = exact/symbolic result with a machine-checked lock; [COMPUTED] = numerical result, precision stated; [CITED] = literature; [CONJECTURE]/[OPEN] = stated as such.

---

## Abstract

Gap labelling — the assignment of the integrated density of states (IDS) on
every spectral gap of an aperiodic Hamiltonian to a countable module of
algebraic numbers — is classical (Bellissard–Bovier–Ghez 1992;
Kaminker–Putnam), but every explicitly worked example in the Schrödinger,
tight-binding, photonic and tiling-K-theory literature realises a module of
degree at most two over ℚ (Fibonacci/Sturmian ℤ+ℤ/φ, silver/octagonal
ℚ(√2), Thue–Morse rationals). We exhibit the first explicit degree-four
instantiation. A primitive four-letter substitution σ₄ has quartic Pisot
Perron eigenvalue β = τ²(1+τ) ≈ 3.6762, where τ = √φ satisfies
τ⁴ − τ² − 1 = 0 (field ℚ(√φ), Galois conjugates of modulus 0.440, 0.786,
0.786). We prove exactly that the letter-frequency module — hence the
predicted gap-label module — is the full ring ℤ[τ]: the four frequencies
f_a = τ−1, f_b = τ³−τ²−τ+1, f_A = τ²−τ, f_B = −τ³+τ+1 have unimodular
coefficient matrix in the basis (1, τ, τ², τ³). An exact Sturm-count IDS at
N = 10⁶ sites confirms the five principal gap labels to 4×10⁻⁷,
coupling-invariantly across two unrelated coupling sets and 189 sampled
coupling points. The labels obey an exact one-measurement law
(f_A = τ·f_a, f_a+f_b = τ²·f_a): one measured label forces all others. We
propose a four-coupling polariton/photonic chain — one patterning step
beyond the fabricated golden chains — as the first measurement able to read
a degree-four label module, state the prediction in the winding-number form
used in the existing polariton experiments, and preregister the falsifiers
and controls. The infinite-volume gap-opening theorem remains open; we
state this boundary precisely.

## 1. Introduction

The gap-labelling theorem is one of the earliest structural results of the
noncommutative geometry of aperiodic media: for a one-dimensional
Schrödinger or tight-binding operator whose couplings are modulated by a
minimal aperiodic subshift, the IDS evaluated on any spectral gap lies in
the image of the trace on K₀ of the associated C*-algebra, which for
substitution subshifts is the ℤ-module generated by the word frequencies
[CITED: Bellissard–Bovier–Ghez 1992; Bellissard 1992, Prop. 5.2.4;
Kaminker–Putnam]. The theorem is alphabet-agnostic and has been stated in
full generality for minimal Cantor systems for over two decades.

Its worked examples, however, have remained resolutely quadratic. The
Fibonacci chain carries labels in ℤ + ℤ/φ (field ℚ(√5)) — the case whose
spectral theory is the deepest developed [CITED: Damanik–Gorodetski–Yessen
2016] and the only case measured in a laboratory: photonic and polaritonic
realisations of the golden chain read the label integers directly as
winding numbers of gap modes under structural (phason) scans [CITED:
Baboux et al. 2017; Kraus–Zilberberg 2012]. The octagonal/Ammann–Beenker
and Penrose tilings give ℚ(√2) and ℚ(√5) modules; Thue–Morse and
period-doubling give rationals; the pinwheel gives a rank-one module. Even
the most recent unified treatment of metallic-mean chains remains rank-two
[CITED: Jagannathan 2026]. A systematic literature pass over the
substitution-tiling K-theory corpus (Kellendonk; Kellendonk–Putnam;
Bellissard–Benedetti–Gambaudo; Moustafa; Sadun; the Damanik–Fillman
survey) found no gap-label or IDS frequency-module computation of degree
greater than two, for any Hamiltonian, theoretical or experimental. The
closest precedent is Kellendonk's symbolic k-letter "A-type Fibonacci"
family, which supplies a general rank-k module formula but is never
numerically instantiated for k ≥ 3 — and at k = 7, where the eigenvalue
happens to be quartic, it is **not** Pisot (a Galois conjugate has modulus
≈ 1.83) [CITED: Kellendonk 1995, 1997]. Degree-four quartic-Pisot examples
do exist in the *diffraction* literature [CITED: Baake–Grimm], but pure-point
diffraction spectra are a different object from Schrödinger gap labels —
an arithmetic cousin, not a precedent.

This letter closes the gap between the general theorem and its worked
degree: we present a four-letter primitive substitution whose Perron
eigenvalue is a quartic Pisot number, prove exactly that its
letter-frequency module is the full ring of a quartic order, verify the
predicted labels numerically to 4×10⁻⁷, and propose the four-coupling
photonic measurement that would carry a degree-four label module for the
first time. The two-coupling boundary of every existing experiment was
never a refusal by nature; nobody had built the degree-four system.

## 2. The substitution and its quartic Pisot data

Let 𝒜 = {a, b, A, B} and define the substitution

    σ₄ :  a ↦ abAAB,   b ↦ aAB,   A ↦ abAB,   B ↦ aA.

Since σ₄(a) begins with a, iterating on a yields an infinite fixed point
w = abAAB aAB abAB abAB aA … ; the tight-binding chain studied below reads
its couplings off w. The incidence (abelianisation) matrix, with M_{ij}
counting occurrences of letter i in σ₄(j) and letter order (a, b, A, B), is

    M₄ = ⎡1 1 1 1⎤
         ⎢1 0 1 0⎥
         ⎢2 1 1 1⎥
         ⎣1 1 1 0⎦ .

**Proposition 1 (quartic Pisot data). [PROVED]**
(i) σ₄ is primitive: every letter occurs in σ₄²(c) for each c ∈ 𝒜
(equivalently M₄² > 0 entrywise).
(ii) The characteristic polynomial of M₄ is x⁴ − 2x³ − 5x² − 4x − 1, and
the eigenvalues are exactly

    β = τ²(1+τ) = 3.6762…,   λ₂ = −1/(1+τ) = −0.4401…,
    λ₃, λ̄₃ = −1/φ ± i/τ³,   |λ₃| = 1/τ = 0.7862…,

where τ = √φ (φ the golden ratio) is the positive root of τ⁴ − τ² − 1 = 0.
(iii) All Galois conjugates of β have modulus < 1 (moduli 0.440, 0.786,
0.786): β is a quartic **Pisot** number, and the splitting data live in the
single degree-four field ℚ(τ) = ℚ[x]/(x⁴ − x² − 1) = ℚ(√φ).

The discriminant of x⁴ − x² − 1 equals the discriminant of the
characteristic polynomial, both −400 = −2⁴·5², confirming that the two
polynomials define the same quartic field [COMPUTED, exact integer
arithmetic]. Note σ₄ is of *non-constant length* (image lengths 5, 3, 4, 2);
this matters for the correct citation of the gap-labelling theorem in
Section 4.

## 3. The frequency module is exactly ℤ[τ]

**Theorem 2 (exact frequency module). [PROVED]**
The letter frequencies of the fixed point of σ₄ are the entries of the
normalised Perron eigenvector of M₄ and are given exactly by

    f_a = τ − 1,
    f_b = τ³ − τ² − τ + 1,
    f_A = τ² − τ,
    f_B = −τ³ + τ + 1,

with f_a + f_b + f_A + f_B = 1 and M₄ f = β f identically modulo
τ⁴ − τ² − 1. The coefficient matrix of (f_a, f_b, f_A, f_B) in the ℤ-basis
(1, τ, τ², τ³) has determinant ±1; hence the frequency ℤ-module is not
merely of rank four but is **exactly the full ring**

    ℤ f_a + ℤ f_b + ℤ f_A + ℤ f_B = ℤ[τ].

Moreover each of the five principal labels

    f_b = 0.1681173890…,  f_B = 0.2138486222…,  f_a = 0.2720196495…,
    f_A = 0.3460143392…,  f_a + f_b = 0.4401370385…

is an algebraic number of exact degree four over ℚ (irreducible quartic
minimal polynomials).

**Corollary 3 (one-measurement law). [PROVED]**
The five labels form a single geometric string with ratio τ:

    f_A = τ · f_a,    f_B = τ · f_b,    f_b = f_a/φ,
    f_a + f_b = τ² · f_a = τ³ − τ² = 1/(1 + τ),

all exact identities in ℚ(τ). Consequently the measurement of any single
label determines every other label with no free parameter. (Verified
internally to 10⁻¹² as a floating-point sanity check; the identities are
symbolic.)

Theorem 2 strengthens the earlier statement that the frequencies are
degree-four: unimodularity means the *measured module and the ring
coincide* — no proper submodule and no denominator escapes. Machine locks:
`tests/test_b560_cells.py` (module = ℤ[τ], det ±1, Perron relation),
`tests/test_b555.py` (one-measurement law, degree-four signature).

## 4. Gap labels: prediction and numerical verification

**Proposition 4 (gap-labelling, general theorem). [CITED]**
For the hopping Hamiltonian (Hψ)_n = t_n ψ_{n+1} + t_{n−1} ψ_{n−1} with
couplings t_n = t_{w_n} read off the fixed point w, the IDS evaluated on
any spectral gap lies in the ℤ-module generated by the word frequencies —
here, by Theorem 2, in ℤ[τ] (mod 1). This is classical: the
one-dimensional gap-labelling mechanism is Bellissard–Bovier–Ghez (1992);
since σ₄ is of non-constant length, the technically applicable statement is
Bellissard's general K-theoretic form and the full-generality proof for
minimal Cantor ℤ-systems (Kaminker–Putnam). *Nothing in Proposition 4 is
ours; our contribution is the degree-four instantiation, its exact module,
and the experiment.*

**Result 5 (numerical verification). [COMPUTED]**
For the chain on w with coupling set (t_a, t_b, t_A, t_B) =
(1.0, 0.8, 0.6, 0.4):

- *Exact Sturm-count IDS at N = 10⁶ sites* (resolution 10⁻⁶), evaluated at
  the twelve largest gap centres, identifies the labels with the ℚ(√φ)
  values of Theorem 2 to **4×10⁻⁷**:

  | label (folded) | identification | deviation |
  |---|---|---|
  | 0.4401370 | f_a + f_b = τ²(τ−1) | 3.9×10⁻⁸ |
  | 0.3460140 | f_A = τ(τ−1) | 3.4×10⁻⁷ |
  | 0.2720200 | f_a = τ−1 | 3.5×10⁻⁷ |
  | 0.2138490 | f_B | 3.8×10⁻⁷ |
  | 0.1681170 | f_b | 3.9×10⁻⁷ |
  | 0.0581700 | τ⁻¹(τ−1)² | 3×10⁻⁷ |

  (Labels are chirality-folded, x ↔ 1−x, using the E ↔ −E symmetry of the
  pure-hopping model.) The sixth label τ⁻¹(τ−1)² ∈ ℤ[τ] lies beyond the
  five principal targets and continues the same geometric τ-string one rung
  down — the measurable module is the ring, as Theorem 2 predicts.

- *Coupling invariance.* The full label set recurs, at the same values, for
  the unrelated coupling set (1.0, 0.55, 0.75, 0.35): at N = 3000
  (diagonalisation, top-12 gaps) both sets hit the dictionary at
  1.4×10⁻⁵–2×10⁻⁴ (resolution 1/N ≈ 3.3×10⁻⁴); at N = 24001 (top-30 gaps)
  the identification margins are 22–59× (f_b at 6×10⁻⁷) and the invariance
  is exact gap-by-gap. Only the gap *widths* (rankings) move with the
  couplings, as gap labelling requires.

- *Coupling-chamber robustness.* On 189 sampled coupling points (a 64-point
  grid in the box b∈[0.72,0.88], A∈[0.52,0.68], B∈[0.32,0.48] and a
  125-point grid in b∈[0.52,0.58], A∈[0.72,0.78], B∈[0.32,0.38], t_a = 1),
  all five target gaps remain among the 30 largest at every point, with
  minimum widths 0.021–0.320.

- *Finite-size scaling.* At substitution-prefix lengths N = 893, 3283,
  12069, 44368 (both benchmark coupling sets, 40 tracked gaps) every target
  gap stays open and the label error obeys the integer-count scale
  |IDS_N − f| = O(1/N), with max N·|IDS_N − f| = 0.129, while the target
  widths stay of order one.

- *Finite-N perturbation budget.* [PROVED, elementary] If every hopping
  changes by at most δ then ‖ΔH‖₂ ≤ 2δ and each gap edge moves by at most
  2δ; a gap of width g survives whenever 4δ < g, and with an experimental
  linewidth Γ the conservative combined budget is 4δ + 2Γ < g_min.

## 5. The experiment: a four-coupling photonic chain

Two-coupling (golden) chains have been fabricated and their gap labels
measured in cavity-polariton lattices; there the label integers (p, q) in
IDS = p + q/φ (mod 1) are read directly, q as the winding number of gap
modes under a structural phason scan [CITED: Baboux et al. 2017; see also
Kraus–Zilberberg 2012 for the pumping form]. Four coupling strengths are
one patterning step beyond this demonstrated capability. We propose:
fabricate the σ₄ chain — assign four coupling amplitudes to the letters of
the fixed point w — and measure the IDS at the major gaps.

**Prediction (winding form).** Every gap label of the σ₄ chain is a
ℤ-vector (p₀, p₁, p₂, p₃) with

    IDS ≡ p₀ + p₁τ + p₂τ² + p₃τ³  (mod 1),

i.e. the label group is rank **four**, and the five principal gaps carry
exactly

    f_a:      (−1, 1, 0, 0)      = 0.2720196495
    f_b:      ( 1, −1, −1, 1)    = 0.1681173890
    f_A:      ( 0, −1, 1, 0)     = 0.3460143392
    f_B:      ( 1, 1, 0, −1)     = 0.2138486222
    f_a+f_b:  ( 0, 0, −1, 1)     = 0.4401370385

(up to the chirality fold x ↔ 1−x). Any two-coupling metallic-mean chain
must reduce to the rank-two label formula [CITED: Jagannathan 2026]; the
four-coupling chain is the minimal system whose label module has rank four.
The one-measurement law of Corollary 3 is the experimental signature:
measure any one label and every other is forced, f_A = τ·f_a and
f_a + f_b = τ²·f_a exactly.

**Design floor.** [COMPUTED] Because ℤ + ℤ/φ is dense mod 1, "distance to
the golden module" is meaningful only at bounded index: for golden labels
P + Q/φ with |Q| ≤ 30, the smallest distance to any of the five quartic
targets is 9.33×10⁻⁴, so the total IDS uncertainty must satisfy
ε_IDS < 4.67×10⁻⁴; counting resolution alone then requires N ≥ 2144, and
we recommend N ≥ 5000 as a conservative floor. A golden chain can
counterfeit the target values only at index |Q| ≈ 763–1998 (10⁻⁴
agreement) or |Q| ≈ 7528–19709 (10⁻⁵) — such high-index labels do not
occur among the largest low-index gaps. The correct discriminator is
therefore: *the labels occur among the largest low-index gaps and obey the
exact quartic relations*, not a naive decimal distance to a dense quadratic
module. (In-silico, N = 24001 already gives 22–59× identification margins.)

**Preregistered falsifiers** (the prediction can lose):

(a) the measured labels drift to ℤ + ℤ/φ as N grows → the object is
    degree-two, not four; the claim dies;
(b) the labels depend on the coupling amplitudes → they are not gap labels
    (would contradict the gap-labelling theorem itself);
(c) the labels match the silver/tribonacci values → wrong metallic member;
(d) f_A ≠ τ · f_a within resolution → not this object.

**Controls** (object-specificity):

- a silver-mean two-coupling chain gives labels in ℤ + ℤ/(1+√2)
  (e.g. 0.4142…), disjoint from the five targets — platform validation;
- a generic-slope chain built at slope 0.44014 (not by inflation) gives
  degree-four labels in a *different* module, ℤ + ℤ(τ³−τ²) — the full ring
  ℤ[τ] is reachable only through the substitution structure, so the module
  identity, not mere quarticity, is what the experiment reads.

## 6. Limitations (honest scope)

1. **The theorem is not ours.** The gap-labelling mechanism — labels lie in
   the frequency module — is classical [CITED: Bellissard–Bovier–Ghez
   1992; Kaminker–Putnam]. Any k-letter instantiation of the *module
   statement* is a corollary of the general theorem. Our claims are
   exactly: (i) the first explicit degree-four, quartic-Pisot gap-label /
   IDS frequency-module instantiation for a substitution tight-binding
   Hamiltonian, with the module identified exactly as ℤ[τ]
   (Theorem 2 [PROVED]) and verified to 4×10⁻⁷ (Result 5 [COMPUTED]);
   (ii) the four-coupling experiment proposal. Nothing more.

2. **The novelty scope is exactly as stated.** A three-pass adversarial
   literature gate (Schrödinger/hopping literature; photonic/polaritonic
   experiments; substitution-tiling K-theory corpus) plus an independent
   second sweep found no gap-label computation or measurement above
   quadratic fields anywhere. The honest near-miss is Kellendonk's k-letter
   A-type family: a general rank-k module *formula*, never numerically
   instantiated for k ≥ 3, and non-Pisot at its quartic point (k = 7,
   conjugate modulus ≈ 1.83) [CITED: Kellendonk 1995, 1997]. Baake–Grimm's
   quartic-Pisot results concern diffraction spectra, a physically distinct
   measurement [CITED]. Should an overlooked degree-four instantiation
   surface, claim (i) reduces to "an independent worked example" and the
   experiment proposal stands on its own.

3. **Gap opening is open.** The gap-labelling theorem predicts *labels*,
   not that every predicted gap opens. Even in the quadratic case the
   "every label is realised by an open gap" statement (the Dry Ten Martini
   problem) was closed for all couplings only in 2024
   [CITED: Band–Beckus–Loewy; large coupling: Raymond 1995]. For σ₄:
   - an infinite-volume theorem that the five target gaps stay open
     throughout a continuous coupling chamber is **[OPEN]** (our evidence
     is 189 finite-N coupling points plus finite-size scaling to
     N = 44368 — strong, not a proof);
   - the degree-four dry-gap statement (every ℤ[τ] label realised) is
     **[OPEN]**;
   - the K-theory computation for the σ₄ algebra (K₀, order structure,
     border-forcing check) is **[OPEN]**, queued as follow-up work.
   The experiment tests the label values on the gaps that do open — it does
   not presuppose the open problems.

4. **A corrected earlier statement.** An earlier internal phrasing, "the
   labels sit 2–3.6×10⁻² off the golden lattice", is meaningful only under
   a coefficient cutoff (|P|,|Q| ≤ 6 there), since ℤ + ℤ/φ is dense mod 1.
   Section 5 states the corrected bounded-index control and the resulting
   design floor. The verdict is unchanged; the phrasing is now exact.

5. **No physical constants are claimed.** The construction predicts
   dimensionless IDS values for a designed device. It does not predict
   coupling constants, mass ratios, or any Standard-Model quantity, and no
   such connection is asserted.

## 7. Outlook

The natural continuations are (i) the K-theory computation for the σ₄
subshift algebra, upgrading the module statement to the full ordered-K₀
label group and checking border-forcing; (ii) an infinite-volume
gap-opening theorem for at least one coupling chamber, for which the
trace-map/transfer-operator technology developed for Fibonacci
[CITED: Damanik–Gorodetski–Yessen 2016] is the obvious toolkit, now needed
at a genuinely quartic Pisot point; (iii) the measurement itself. The
platform exists, the patterning step is incremental, and the prediction is
maximally exposed: five numbers, four integers per gap, one exact internal
law, four ways to lose. K-theoretic label integers are, moreover,
boundary-readable in the standard bulk-boundary correspondence
[CITED: Prodan–Schulz-Baldes], suggesting an edge-mode route complementary
to the IDS count.
