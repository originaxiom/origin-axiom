### [MAJOR] papers/P4_markov_stage/DRAFT_v1.md §2.2 (lines 124-153) and Abstract (lines 14-19)
- **claim:** Headline Theorem A: tr[A_m,A_n] = 2 − (mn(n−m))², 'the deviation from parabolicity is a perfect square', unique parabolic pair (1,2).
- **attack:** The perfect square is not a property of the metallic family — it is a one-line generic identity for ALL pairs of symmetric SL(2,R) matrices. Since A and B symmetric gives BA = (AB)^T, the commutator is [A,B] = M·M^{−T} with M = AB, and tr(M·M^{−T}) = 2 − (M₁₂ − M₂₁)² identically (I verified this symbolically and on 50 random symmetric integer SL(2) pairs). The metallic matrices are symmetric; M₁₂ − M₂₁ = mn(m−n) is the only family-specific content. Any symmetric family whatsoever has 'quantized tears' and 'equal squares are equal tears'. The paper proves the identity by blind polynomial expansion ('verified by expansion... 9×9 integer grid'), which both hides the mechanism and dresses a structural triviality as a discovered miracle. The uniqueness clause is then literally 'the only way to factor 2' — a triviality about small integers, exactly as suspected. The genuinely interesting statement is not the square; it is the identification with Cohn's pair (Theorem B).
- **fix:** Reprove Theorem A in two lines via the transpose identity, credit the symmetry (transpose involution = the elliptic/hyperelliptic involution acting on the punctured-torus character variety — this is why Cohn's matrices are symmetric), and move the paper's significance weight from 'the square' to 'the family's parabolic locus is exactly Cohn's stage'. This also changes the novelty gate for §5 item (a): search the symmetric-matrix/palindrome corner of Markov theory, where this identity very plausibly already lives.
