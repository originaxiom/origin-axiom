## §0 Abstract
The metallic matrices A_m = [[m²+1, m],[m, 1]] (m = 1: golden, the figure-eight knot's
monodromy) satisfy tr[A_m, A_n] = 2 − (mn(n−m))²: the commutator trace's deviation from
parabolicity is a perfect square, and exactly one pair — (1,2) — closes the cusp. That
pair generates Cohn's modular torus; the substitution walks the Markov spine with the
cusp intact at every stage. The family is characterized by breathability (tr − 2 = m² ⟺
an orientation-reversing half); the norm of the metallic mean is −1, uniformly. At the
level defined by the pair's own trace (15), the quantized commutator is governed by two
characters (a quaternionic parity and a mod-5 closure bit), the quantum cusp closes at
the CRT-central address [W₁², W₂³] = I, and the entire selection architecture of the
pair's interaction factors through the divisor lattice of the two clock orders.
