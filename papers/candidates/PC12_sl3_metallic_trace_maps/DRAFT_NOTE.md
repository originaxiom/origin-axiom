# Metallic SL(3) Trace Maps: Commutator Invariants, Entropy, and Fixed-Line Arithmetic

Status: short computational note prepared for external mathematical review. Not a
public preprint. Pure character-variety / algebraic-dynamics mathematics; it is
intended to be read on its own terms.

## Abstract

Let `F_2 = <a,b>` and, for an integer `m >= 1`, let `phi_m` be the free-group
automorphism

```text
phi_m(a) = a^m b,    phi_m(b) = a.
```

Its abelianization is the metallic companion matrix `M = [[m,1],[1,0]]`
(`char M = t^2 - m t - 1`, Perron root the metallic mean
`mu_m = (m + sqrt(m^2+4))/2`). We record the polynomial trace map `T_m` that
`phi_m` induces on the rank-two `SL(3,C)` character variety in Lawton's minimal
trace coordinates, and collect four elementary facts about it:

1. an explicit eight-coordinate Cayley-Hamilton form for `T_m` (Section 2);
2. invariance of the unordered commutator trace pair
   `{tr[A,B], tr[A,B]^{-1}}` (Section 3);
3. algebraic entropy `h(T_m) = log mu_m` (Section 4);
4. along the diagonal fixed line, an exchange-symmetry factorization of the
   linearization (Section 5) and an integer-splitting classification of the
   antisymmetric sector quartic (Section 6), plus the surviving compact
   `SU(3)` diagonal slice (Section 7).

Facts 1-3 and the exchange factorization are standard methods (Lawton; Horowitz;
Procesi; Baake-Grimm-Roberts; Bellon-Viallet) applied to this family. The
integer-splitting classification (Section 6) is the one component we have not
located in the literature; confirming or placing it is the main purpose of an
external read. An appendix records a *numerical* higher-rank (`SL(n)`)
continuation that is explicitly not part of the theorem package.

## 1. Setup and coordinates

For a pair `(A,B) in SL(3,C)^2`, the rank-two `SL(3,C)` character variety is
generated by nine trace coordinates and is a hypersurface in `C^9`
(Lawton 2008, arXiv:0709.4403; algebraic independence in arXiv:0807.0798;
classical reduction theory in Procesi and Horowitz). We use the eight generators

```text
x1 = tr A      x2 = tr B      x3 = tr AB     x4 = tr A^{-1}
x5 = tr B^{-1} x6 = tr A^{-1}B x7 = tr A B^{-1} x8 = tr A^{-1}B^{-1}
```

together with the two-valued commutator coordinate `{tr[A,B], tr[A,B]^{-1}}`
(the ninth generator and its conjugate; the hypersurface relation expresses the
pair through `x1..x8`). The automorphism `phi_m` acts on `SL(3,C)^2` by
`(A,B) -> (phi_m(A), phi_m(B)) = (A^m B,\ A)` and hence on the coordinate ring;
the induced polynomial map is the trace map `T_m`.

## 2. The metallic trace map (standard)

Write `tau_k = tr(A^k B)` and `sigma_k = tr(A^k B^{-1})`. The Cayley-Hamilton
relation for `A in SL(3)` (`A^3 = x1 A^2 - x4 A + I`) gives the third-order
recurrences

```text
tau_k   = x1 tau_{k-1} - x4 tau_{k-2} + tau_{k-3},
sigma_k = x4 sigma_{k-1} - x1 sigma_{k-2} + sigma_{k-3},
```

with initial data `tau_{-1}=x6, tau_0=x2, tau_1=x3` and
`sigma_{-1}=x7, sigma_0=x5, sigma_1=x8`. In these coordinates

```text
T_m = ( tau_m, x1, tau_{m+1}, sigma_m, x4, sigma_{m-1}, tau_{m-1}, sigma_{m+1} ).
```

(For `m=1`: `T_1 = (x3, x1, x1 x3 - x4 x2 + x6, x8, x4, x5, x2,
x4 x8 - x1 x5 + x7)`.)

This is the standard induced-trace-map construction: the coordinate ring is
Lawton's, and the explicit recurrences are Cayley-Hamilton reduction. Trace maps
of substitutions above `SL(2)` already appear in the lattice literature
(e.g. the `N`-ary Fibonacci trace map, Phys. Lett. A, 1994).

## 3. Commutator trace-pair invariant (standard)

The unordered pair `{tr[A,B], tr[A,B]^{-1}}` is invariant under `T_m`. This
reduces to the group identity `[a^m b, a] \sim [a,b]^{-1}` (conjugacy in `F_2`);
the action of an automorphism on the commutator coordinate is standard
mapping-class / character-variety theory.

## 4. Algebraic entropy (standard)

The Bellon-Viallet algebraic entropy of `T_m` (the exponential growth rate of
the iterated coordinate degrees) is

```text
h(T_m) = log mu_m = log( (m + sqrt(m^2+4)) / 2 ).
```

The degrees grow by the metallic substitution recurrence `d_{k+1} = m d_k +
d_{k-1}`; absence of cancellation makes the growth exactly the Perron root of
`M = [[m,1],[1,0]]`, i.e. `mu_m`. This is the standard Bellon-Viallet mechanism
(degree growth is exponential precisely when leading terms do not cancel); for
mapping-class actions on character varieties the algebraic entropy equals the
spectral radius of the induced action (arXiv:0812.0853), here `rho(M) = mu_m`.

## 5. Fixed-line linearization and exchange sectors

The diagonal locus where all eight coordinates equal a common value `c` is
invariant; call it the fixed line. Write the linearization (Jacobian) of `T_m`
there as `J(m,c)`. The exchange involution

```text
P:  x1<->x4, x2<->x5, x3<->x8, x6<->x7
```

is a reversing symmetry: `[J(m,c), P] = 0` for all `m` and `c` (verified
symbolically; see Appendix B). Block-diagonalizing on the `P`-eigenspaces splits
`J(m,c)` into a symmetric and an antisymmetric sector. At the distinguished point
`c = 3` (the triple-root point, where `A=B` has characteristic polynomial
`(t-1)^3`) the derivative rows are polynomial in `m`, and the sector
characteristic polynomials are

```text
symmetric:      (t-1)(t+1)(t^2 - (m^2+2) t + 1),
antisymmetric:  (t^2 + m t - 1)(t^2 - (m^3+3m) t - 1).
```

These factor through the metallic companion `M`: with `L_k = tr(M^k)`
(`L_0=2, L_1=m, L_{k}=m L_{k-1}+L_{k-2}`, so `L_2=m^2+2`, `L_3=m^3+3m`) and
`det M^k = (-1)^k`,

```text
t^2-(m^2+2)t+1 = char(M^2),   t^2+mt-1 = char(M^{-1}),   t^2-(m^3+3m)t-1 = char(M^3),
```

so the `c=3` fixed-line linearization is

```text
(t-1)(t+1) · char(M^{-1}) · char(M^2) · char(M^3)        (all m >= 1).
```

The block decomposition is the Baake-Grimm-Roberts reversing-symmetry framework
(arXiv:math/9901124); the specific `char(M^k)` factorization for this family is
the structural fact that Section 6 and Appendix A build on.

## 6. Fixed-line integer-splitting classification

Away from `c=3`, the antisymmetric sector is a quartic which, after normalization
by its palindromic symmetry, has the form

```text
chi(t) = t^4 - A t^3 + C t^2 + A t + 1,
```

with `A, C` integer polynomials in `(m,c)`.

**Splitting criterion.** `chi` factors as `(t^2 - alpha t - 1)(t^2 - beta t - 1)`
with `alpha, beta in Z` if and only if

```text
D = A^2 - 4(C+2)  is a perfect square   and   A + sqrt(D)  is even.
```

**Classification.** Over `Z`, `chi` splits in exactly the following cases:

```text
c = 1,  c = 3            (all m)
c = 0   with 3 | m
c = 2   with 6 | m
c = -1  with 2 | m
c = -3  with m = 2, 3
c = -9, -11  with m = 1.
```

The proof is assembled from reusable modules: the criterion above; the listed
split families and isolated cases; a square-gap propagation lemma
(`R^2 < D < (R+1)^2` kept by third-difference recurrence propagation); finite
positive-strip tables for `4 <= c <= 14`; negative-strip and boundary tables for
`-11 <= c <= -2`; and global coefficient-positivity exclusions for `c >= 15` and
`c <= -12`. The `c=3` block is proved symbolically (Section 5). This elementary
Diophantine classification is the component not located in the literature.

## 7. Compact SU(3) diagonal slice

Among the integer splitting values, the compact diagonal `SU(3)` slice (diagonal
representatives with unitary entries) realizes precisely

```text
c = -1, 0, 1, 3,
```

by explicit representatives together with a centrality exclusion for `c = 2`.
This is a compact-unitary restriction of the trace dynamics.

## 8. Non-claims

This note makes no physical interpretation of any kind. In particular:

```text
- The compact SU(3) slice (Section 7) is compact-unitary linear algebra, not a
  gauge theory.
- The direct/inverse trace distinction (e.g. tau vs sigma) is a formal
  coordinate distinction, not a physical one.
- A naive three-channel Fibonacci tight-binding transfer model does NOT realize
  T_m: standard multichannel transfer matrices are doubled (6x6 symplectic),
  which do not satisfy the third-order SL(3) trace recurrence of Section 2.
- The diagonal SL(2,C) figure-eight reps do not lie on the surface I = 1/4: they
  give Fricke-Vogt invariant I in {4, -17/2 +- 7 sqrt(5)/2}, none equal to 1/4.
```

## Appendix A — A numerical higher-rank continuation (NOT a theorem)

This appendix records a numerical observation and is explicitly **not** part of
the theorem package above. It is included only to frame a question for a
specialist (see the review packet).

The construction of Sections 1-2 generalizes verbatim to `SL(n,C)`: the rank-two
`SL(n,C)` character variety has dimension `n^2-1`, and `phi_m` induces a trace
map whose linearization at the diagonal fixed line can be computed. For the
Fibonacci case `m = 1` (`M = [[1,1],[1,0]]`, golden) we computed the fixed-line
multiplier spectrum numerically, by extrapolating an ambient Jacobian
`DT(eps) = D[tr W_i(phi(A,B))] · pinv( D[tr W_j(A,B)] )` from perturbed
representations `A=exp(eps P), B=exp(eps Q)` to `eps -> 0`, with the spectrum
identified against `char(M^k) = t^2 - L_k t + (-1)^k`, sign sectors
`char(-M^k) = t^2 + L_k t + (-1)^k`, and a parity block `(t-1)^a (t+1)^b`. The
method reproduces the `SL(2)` and `SL(3)` (Section 5, `m=1`) factorizations to
high precision.

Observed continuation (`m = 1`):

```text
n   M-powers present        sign sectors    parity block        status
2   {2}                     none            none                exact
3   {-1, 2, 3}              none            (t-1)(t+1)           exact (Section 5)
4   {-1, 1, 2, 3, 4}        {-2}            (t-1)^2(t+1)         over Z[m], all m (B63)
5   {-1, 1, 1, 2, 3, 4, 5}  {-2, -3}        (t-1)^2(t+1)^2       numerical (22 of 24)
```

(For `n=4` the factorization is established for general `m` over `Z[m]` by a
computer-assisted high-precision computation -- the factor sums interpolate to
`L_k = tr(M^k)`, e.g. `L_2=m^2+2, L_3=m^3+3m, L_4=m^4+4m^2+2` -- showing the
M-power/sign/parity structure is m-independent. Moreover the entire `15x15`
fixed-line Jacobian `J(m)` is reconstructed exactly over `Z[m]` (its entries are
canonical degree-4 polynomials in `m`, fixed by an over-determined fit) and
`char(J(m))` is then *factored directly* over `Z[m]` into the above product --
the factorization derived, not matched. The entries are computer-determined; a
hand-derived Procesi trace-ring proof remains the purist open item.)

(`n=3` is the `m=1` specialization of Section 5: `(t-1)(t+1)·char(M^{-1})·
char(M^2)·char(M^3)`.) For `n=5`, 22 of the 24 multipliers resolve to the catalog
at 60-digit precision; the remaining 2 lie in the coordinate directions where the
trace-coordinate differential loses rank at the fixed line, where the
pseudoinverse is discontinuous and the `eps -> 0` limit becomes dependent on the
perturbation direction (it scatters across random `P,Q`). Those two are not
recoverable by this representation-perturbation method and would require a
symbolic ambient `SL(n,C)` trace-ring computation.

A structural identification of those two modes: under the hypothesis that the
exchange involution (`tr W <-> tr W^{-1}`) is the opposition involution
`theta = -w_0` on the `sl(n)` root system, its eigenspace split on the height-2
root space is `char(M^2)^2 . char(-M^2)` for `n=5` (and reproduces `n=3,4`
exactly). As four of those six dimensions are resolved above as
`char(M^2) . char(-M^2)`, the two remaining modes would be a second `char(M^2)`,
i.e. eigenvalues `phi^2, 1/phi^2` (consistent with the residual modes being
positive). This identification is structural, not proved here; it is one of the
questions for a specialist (see the review packet, Q4).

The sector *assignment* itself does have an exact-algebra explanation. The
fixed-line Jacobian commutes with `P`, and three facts -- depth-`n`
Cayley-Hamilton (Jacobian polynomial in `m`), `P` = contragredient (`m -> -m`),
and Dickson parity `L_k(-m) = (-1)^k L_k(m)` -- force the `P`-symmetric sector's
characteristic polynomial to be even in `m` and the `P`-antisymmetric one odd.
Hence `char(M^k)` with even `|k|` lies in the symmetric sector and odd `|k|` in
the antisymmetric one. This is verified in full symbolic-`m` form for `n=3`
(symmetric `(t-1)(t+1)char(M^2)`, antisymmetric `char(M^-1)char(M^3)`) and assigns
the `n=4,5` factors accordingly.

This appendix is a numerical, method-validated observation only — **not** a
proven theorem and not part of the classification of Sections 2-7.
