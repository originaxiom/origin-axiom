## 5. Credibility assessment

**Strengths.** The mathematics that is claimed as proven is correct and independently
checkable. The project repeatedly kills its own best ideas — that is rare and real.
The V4/Reality-Check discipline (explicit "claims made / claims not made" lists, a graded
claim ledger, red-flag sections) is genuinely good scientific hygiene.

**Risks.**
1. *Framing drift.* The same structure gets re-narrated as cosmology every few sessions and
   re-killed every few sessions. The new repo must lock the framing so it stops drifting.
2. *Chosen axioms.* The whole edifice rests on the minimal record axioms A1–A6, which were
   selected to yield `L,R`. This is acknowledged in the Reality Check but remains the
   load-bearing weakness.
3. *No observational contact.* Every quantity that touches a real measurement either is a
   fit (θ\*) or was killed (`k≈137`, dynamic `Λ`). The honest anchors (`log2`, `log3`,
   `log φ²`, `ξ=1/log3`) live in toy/transfer models.
4. *Topic-name liability.* The Chatterpillar transcript opens in conspiracy-theory territory.
   That has zero bearing on the mathematics, but for *credibility with outside readers* the
   new repo should be self-contained and reference only the math, never that lineage.

**Verdict.** Treat this as what the project's own best document calls it: a
*candidate classical/statistical transfer-matrix framework with topological and Lorentzian
mathematical structure*. Build on that. Let the physics ambition be an explicitly-labelled
research frontier, not a claimed result.
